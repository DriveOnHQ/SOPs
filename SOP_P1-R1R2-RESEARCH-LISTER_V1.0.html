2. Role Purpose & Context

Why this dual role exists in the CaniCol model and how it supports the lean-volume testing engine.

Core Purpose

The P1-R1R2-RESEARCH-LISTER role represents an early-stage "T-shaped" pattern where one operator handles both:

  • Research Intelligence (R1/R2): Conducting initial plausibility checks and deep product research to advance packets through the pipeline
  • Store Listing Implementation: Translating research-enriched product data into live Shopify product listings

Why This Role Exists

This dual role serves critical economic and operational functions:

  • Pipeline Velocity: Maintains continuous flow from research to listing without handoff delays in lean team structures
  • Context Preservation: The same person who researched the product creates the listing, reducing information loss
  • Resource Efficiency: Maximizes utilization of research talent during listing bottlenecks
  • Quality Control: Research insights directly inform listing quality (descriptions, features, positioning)

CaniCol Model Alignment

This role directly supports the CaniCol v4.0 philosophy:

  • Lean-Volume Testing: Enables 45-60 products/month to flow through R1‚ÜíR2‚ÜíR3‚ÜíListing‚ÜíP1 without bottlenecks
  • Budget Discipline: Research enrichment improves listing quality without adding separate FTE costs
  • Hit Rate Focus: Better listings (informed by research) improve P1 test validity and reduce false negatives
  • Portfolio Thinking: High throughput across both research and listing maintains statistical power
üí° Long-Term Evolution Path

Per L2-03 canon: This dual-role pattern is explicitly early-stage. As the team scales, best practice is to decompose into separate specialized roles (pure researchers + pure listers) to maximize depth and efficiency.

Connection to Holdings & Brand (L0/L1)

This role upholds DriveOn's commitment to:

  • Systems-First Execution: Structured R1/R2 research prevents random product selection
  • Anti-Hype Discipline: Research-backed listings avoid exaggerated claims
  • Runnable Operations: Clear SOP enables junior execution without founder heroics

Non-negotiable rules from L0-L3 canon that govern this role's decisions and workflows.

Economics & Testing Volume (from L1-01, L2-05)

  • Testing Volume: Support pipeline velocity of ~45-60 products/month reaching P1/P2
  • P1 Budget: $40-$60 per product (research must optimize for testability within this constraint)
  • P1+P2 Total: $70-$120 standard band (listings must support efficient testing)
  • Test Bank: ‚âà$4,000-$5,500/month total P1+P2 spend (research throughput must align)
  • Winner Expectations: 4-6% true winners (‚âà2-4 products/month) ‚Äì most research leads to kills

WIP Limits & Throughput (from L2-03)

For 1.0 FTE allocation:

  • R1 Active: ‚â§ 6 packets in progress
  • R2 Active: ‚â§ 3 packets (deep work constraint)
  • R3-Prep: ‚â§ 1 packet awaiting final handoff
  • Listing Tasks: 4-6 in progress simultaneously

FTE Split Scaling:

If operating at 0.6 research / 0.4 listing split:

  • R1 Active: ceil(6 √ó 0.6) = 4 packets
  • R2 Active: ceil(3 √ó 0.6) = 2 packets
  • R3-Prep: 1 packet
  • Listing Tasks: ceil(5 √ó 0.4) = 2 tasks

Weekly Throughput Targets (1.0 FTE):

  • Research: 3-4 R3-ready packets/week
  • Listing: 15-20 researched listings/week (3-5 listings/day)
  • Monthly: 60-80 listings/month
‚ö†Ô∏è WIP Violations Are Signals

Per L2-03: WIP breaches trigger process/capacity review, not automatic inflation. If you consistently hit WIP caps, escalate to Lead Strategist and Project Manager for workload rebalancing or capacity planning.

Pass/Kill Criteria (from L2-05)

While R1/R2 research doesn't make final pass/kill decisions, you must understand P1 thresholds to inform research quality:

  • P1 Pass Indicators: Strong CTR, reasonable CPC, depth events (ATC/IC/Purchase)
  • P1 Kill Indicators: Weak hook (<1% CTR), high CPC (>$2), no depth events
  • Research Responsibility: R2 dossiers must surface angle/hook quality to predict P1 viability

Handoff Rules (from L2-02, L2-03)

  • R3 Handoff: Only R3-approved packets enter P1 testing ‚Äì you cannot bypass R3 gate
  • Listing Handoff: Listings must be complete and QA-checked before handoff to P3 store ops

5. Core Workflows

Step-by-step execution guides for all major tasks in this dual role.

Workflow 1: R1 Plausibility Check (Fast Filter)

Trigger: R0 scout or sourcing specialist assigns R1 candidate to you in ClickUp

Time Budget: 15-20 minutes per product

Goal: Quick go/no-go filter ‚Äì eliminate obvious fails before deeper R2 investment

1 Load Product Context
  • Open ClickUp task in "P1 Product Pipeline ‚Üí Research ‚Üí R1 Shortlist"
  • Open linked Notion Product OS page (ProductID: PRD-YYYYMMDD-XXX)
  • Review: Product name, category, initial notes from scout
Tools: ClickUp, Notion
2 Demand Signal Check
  • Quick TikTok search: Is product trending? Any viral videos?
  • Meta Ad Library: Active ads for this product or close variants?
  • Google Trends: Is search volume present or growing?
Pass Criteria: Clear evidence of demand (videos, ads, search volume)
Kill Signal: Zero social proof, no ads, declining trends
Tools: TikTok, Meta Ad Library, Google Trends
3 Red Flag Scan
  • Policy risks: Adult content? Regulated category? Restricted on Meta/TikTok?
  • Logistics risks: Fragile? Hazmat? Oversized shipping?
  • Quality risks: Scammy reviews? Obvious knockoff? Safety complaints?
Kill Signal: Any hard-stop red flag (policy violation, unshippable, dangerous)
Tools: Amazon reviews, AliExpress product pages, common sense
4 Basic Supplier Viability
  • AliExpress/CJ quick search: Product available? Reasonable MOQ? Ship to target markets?
  • Ballpark pricing: Can we hit 2-3x markup and stay competitive?
Pass Criteria: Supplier exists, pricing makes sense (landed cost allows margin)
Kill Signal: No suppliers, pricing impossible (too high landed cost for market)
Tools: AliExpress, CJ Dropshipping, quick calculator
5 Decision & Documentation
  • Update Notion Product OS: Add R1 notes (1-3 sentences on decision)
  • Update ClickUp status:
    • R1_APPROVED ‚Üí Move to "R2 Deep Research" list
    • R1_REJECTED ‚Üí Move to "Killed" list, add kill reason in comment
  • Log kill patterns in Notion for learning (e.g., "No supplier at margin")
Tools: Notion, ClickUp

Quality Bar: Are we only advancing products with plausible demand + no obvious showstoppers?

Output Artifacts: R1 decision logged, ClickUp status updated, Notion notes recorded

Workflow 2: R2 Deep Research & Dossier Creation

Trigger: Product approved at R1, assigned to you for R2 deep dive

Time Budget: 45-90 minutes per product (varies by complexity)

Goal: Build comprehensive dossier answering: "Why will this work? What's the hook?"

1 Research Setup
  • Open ClickUp task in "P1 Product Pipeline ‚Üí Research ‚Üí R2 Deep Research"
  • Open Notion Packet page (PacketID: PCT-PRD-YYYYMMDD-XXX-R2NN)
  • Create Drive folder: /02_P1_Product_Intel/Products/[ProductID]_[ShortName]/01_Research_Raw
  • Open research tools: Minea, PiPiAds, TikTok Creative Center, Meta Ad Library
2 Competitor Mapping
  • Find 3-5 direct competitors selling same/similar product
  • For each competitor, document:
    • Store URL
    • Price point
    • Positioning angle (e.g., "solves X pain point")
    • Key product features emphasized
  • Take screenshots, save to Drive Research_Raw folder
  • Identify price band: Low ($XX-$XX), Mid ($XX-$XX), High ($XX-$XX)
Tools: Google, Shopify stores, screenshots to Drive
3 Angle & Hook Discovery (CRITICAL)
  • Watch 10-15 winning ad creatives (Minea/PiPiAds/TikTok Creative Center)
  • For each strong ad, extract:
    • Hook: First 3-5 seconds of ad (what grabs attention?)
    • Angle: The "why you need this" story (pain point, benefit, transformation)
    • Format: UGC? B-roll? Before/after? Demo?
  • Identify 2-3 Primary Angles with strongest market proof (most views, engagement)
  • Document in Notion Packet page under "Angles & Hooks" section
Quality Check: Can you clearly articulate "This product works because [angle] hooks [audience]"?
Tools: Minea, PiPiAds, TikTok Creative Center, Meta Ad Library
4 Risk Assessment
  • Review negative reviews (Amazon, AliExpress, Reddit)
  • Identify common complaints: Quality issues? Fit problems? Delivery delays?
  • Platform policy check: Any risk of ad rejection? (Health claims, before/after, etc.)
  • Logistics risks: Breakage? Returns rate? Customs issues?
  • Document top 2-3 risks in Notion with severity (Low/Med/High)
Decision Point: Are risks manageable or showstoppers?
5 Supplier Deep Dive
  • Find 2-3 supplier options (AliExpress, CJ, Alibaba)
  • For each supplier, document:
    • Supplier name/URL
    • Unit cost
    • MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)
    • Shipping options & costs
    • Lead time
    • Quality indicators (reviews, photos, certifications)
  • Calculate landed cost (unit + shipping) and required retail price for 2.5-3x margin
  • Update Notion "Supplier Options" section with shortlist
Tools: AliExpress, CJ Dropshipping, Google Sheets (cost calculator)
6 AOV & Economics Estimate
  • Estimate AOV (Average Order Value): Single product price or bundle potential?
  • Estimate margin: (Retail Price - Landed Cost) / Retail Price
  • Sanity check against P1 budget: Can we test at $40-60 and get meaningful data?
  • Document in Notion "Economics" section
7 Dossier Assembly & R3 Prep
  • Ensure Notion Packet page includes ALL required sections:
    • ‚úÖ Product Overview (name, category, ProductID)
    • ‚úÖ Competitor Mapping (3-5 competitors with URLs, pricing)
    • ‚úÖ Angles & Hooks (2-3 primary angles with evidence)
    • ‚úÖ Risk Assessment (top 2-3 risks with severity)
    • ‚úÖ Supplier Options (2-3 suppliers with costs, MOQ, lead time)
    • ‚úÖ Economics Estimate (AOV, margin, P1 budget fit)
    • ‚úÖ References (links to winning ads, competitor stores, reviews)
  • Update ClickUp status: R2_IN_PROGRESS ‚Üí R2_REVIEW
  • Notify Lead Strategist in Discord #p1-r2-dossiers (see Handoff section)

Quality Bar:

  • Can P2 Creative Director read this dossier and create briefs without questions?
  • Does dossier clearly answer: "Why will this product sell?" and "What's the hook?"
  • Are all risks surfaced (not hidden)?

Output Artifacts:

  • Complete Notion R2 Packet page
  • Drive folder with screenshots, research notes
  • ClickUp task at R2_REVIEW status
  • Discord handoff notification

Workflow 3: Product Listing Creation & Implementation

Trigger: R3-approved packet ready for listing, assigned to you in ClickUp P3 listing queue

Time Budget: 30-45 minutes per listing (validation store) or 60-90 minutes (micro branded store)

Goal: Create complete, research-enriched Shopify product listing ready for P1 testing

1 Listing Setup & Context Load
  • Open ClickUp task in "P3 Media & Store ‚Üí Listings ‚Üí To Do"
  • Open linked Notion Product OS page (ProductID: PRD-YYYYMMDD-XXX)
  • Review R3 packet: angles, positioning, product features, supplier info
  • Confirm target store: Validation/micro store? Which Shopify store URL?
  • Gather creative assets: Product images from P2 or Drive folder
Tools: ClickUp, Notion, Drive, Shopify Admin
2 Create Shopify Product Listing
  • Log into Shopify Admin ‚Üí Products ‚Üí Add Product
  • Product Title: Clear, benefit-focused (use primary angle from research)
  • Product Description:
    • Write 3-5 paragraphs (150-250 words) based on R2 angle discovery
    • Structure: Hook (pain point) ‚Üí Features ‚Üí Benefits ‚Üí Social proof ‚Üí CTA
    • Use research insights to avoid generic copy
    • Follow L2-08 brand guardrails (no hype, no exaggerated claims)
  • Product Images:
    • Upload 4-8 high-quality images from P2 assets or supplier
    • Order: Main product shot, lifestyle/in-use, detail shots, size/scale
    • Ensure images load fast (compress if needed)
Tools: Shopify Admin, Google Docs (for description drafting)
3 Pricing & Variants Configuration
  • Pricing:
    • Set price based on R2 economics estimate (ensure 2.5-3x margin)
    • Compare-at price: If using, must be realistic (not fake inflated price)
    • Cross-check with competitor pricing from R2 research
  • Variants:
    • Add all size/color/style variants from supplier
    • Ensure SKUs are unique and trackable
    • Set inventory policy: "Continue selling when out of stock" (for dropship)
4 Collections & Navigation
  • Assign product to appropriate collection(s):
    • Category collection (e.g., "Home & Garden", "Fitness")
    • Testing collection (e.g., "P1 Active Tests" for validation stores)
  • Set product type and vendor tags for filtering
5 SEO & Meta Fields
  • Meta Title: 50-60 characters, include primary keyword
  • Meta Description: 120-150 characters, benefit-focused
  • URL Handle: Clean, keyword-rich slug (auto-generated is usually fine)
  • Note: For validation stores, SEO is secondary (P1 tests are paid traffic)
6 Page Builder Implementation (If Applicable)
  • If using GemPages/PageFly/Shrine custom PDP:
    • Select appropriate template (validation vs branded)
    • Add: Hero image, feature grid, trust badges, FAQ section, reviews placeholder
    • Ensure mobile responsiveness (preview on mobile)
    • CTA buttons: Clear, contrasting color, "Add to Cart" primary action
  • If using default Shopify theme: Ensure theme settings are optimized (images, fonts, layout)
Tools: GemPages, PageFly, or Shopify theme editor
7 QA & Pre-Launch Checklist
  • ‚úÖ Product title clear and benefit-focused?
  • ‚úÖ Description reflects R2 research angles?
  • ‚úÖ Pricing correct (margin check)?
  • ‚úÖ All variants configured?
  • ‚úÖ Images load correctly on desktop + mobile?
  • ‚úÖ Collections assigned correctly?
  • ‚úÖ Shipping settings correct (check Shopify Shipping profiles)?
  • ‚úÖ No broken links or missing sections?
Action: Test "Add to Cart" flow from PDP ‚Üí Cart ‚Üí Checkout (at least on staging)
8 Go Live & Documentation
  • Set product status: Active (visible on store)
  • Update Notion Product OS:
    • Add Shopify product URL
    • Set status: "Listing Live"
    • Note any deviations from R3 plan
  • Update ClickUp: Move task to "Listing Complete" and add product URL in comment
  • Post handoff notification in Discord #p3-stores-validation (see Handoff section)

Quality Bar:

  • Does listing clearly communicate value proposition from R2 research?
  • Is listing error-free (no typos, broken images, wrong pricing)?
  • Can P3 Media Buyer launch P1 ads immediately without listing questions?

Output Artifacts:

  • Live Shopify product listing (URL)
  • Updated Notion Product OS page with listing URL
  • ClickUp task marked complete
  • Discord handoff notification

Ready-to-Copy AI Prompts

Use these prompts with ChatGPT, Claude, or similar tools. Always edit outputs before using in production.

Prompt 1: Competitor Copy Analysis

I'm researching [PRODUCT NAME/CATEGORY] for a dropshipping test.

Here are 5 competitor store pages:
1. [URL or paste copy]
2. [URL or paste copy]
3. [URL or paste copy]
4. [URL or paste copy]
5. [URL or paste copy]

Task: Analyze these competitors and identify:
1. Common positioning angles (what pain point / benefit do they emphasize?)
2. Price range and pricing strategy
3. Key features they all mention vs unique differentiators
4. Copy tone (urgent/scarcity vs calm/informative)

Format: Bullet list, keep it concise (‚â§ 200 words total).

Important: I will verify your findings ‚Äì do not make up patterns that aren't there.

Prompt 2: Angle Extraction from Ads

I watched 10 winning TikTok/Meta ads for [PRODUCT NAME].

Here are the hooks I observed:
1. "[First 5 seconds of ad 1]"
2. "[First 5 seconds of ad 2]"
3. "[First 5 seconds of ad 3]"
... [continue for all ads]

Task: Group these hooks into 2-3 primary angle categories.

For each angle category, provide:
- Angle name (e.g., "Pain Point ‚Üí Solution", "Social Proof", "Transformation")
- 1-2 sentence description of the angle
- Which hooks fit this angle (list the hook numbers)

Format: Numbered list, keep it under 150 words total.

Constraints:
- Do NOT invent angles that aren't clearly present in the hooks
- Focus on what's working in the market, not what "should" work

Prompt 3: Negative Review Summary

I'm researching [PRODUCT NAME] and need to understand quality/risk issues.

Here are 10-15 negative reviews (2-star or lower):
[Paste review text or key complaints]

Task: Summarize the top 3-5 complaint themes.

For each theme, provide:
- Brief description (1 sentence)
- How many reviews mentioned this (rough count)
- Severity rating: Low / Medium / High

Format: Bullet list, under 150 words.

Important: Only report what's actually in the reviews ‚Äì don't speculate about complaints not mentioned.

Prompt 4: Product Description Drafting (Listing Copy)

I need a Shopify product description for [PRODUCT NAME].

Context from research:
- Primary Angle: [Paste angle from R2 dossier]
- Key Features: [List 3-5 features]
- Target Audience: [Brief description]
- Price Point: $XX.XX

Task: Write a product description (150-250 words) that:
1. Opens with a hook addressing the pain point
2. Lists 3-5 key features with benefits (not just specs)
3. Includes light social proof language (e.g., "thousands of customers love...")
4. Ends with clear CTA ("Add to Cart" or "Get Yours Today")

Tone: Clear, benefit-focused, no hype or exaggerated claims.

Constraints:
- No fake urgency ("Only 3 left!")
- No unverifiable claims ("Scientifically proven...")
- No overpromising ("Change your life overnight")
- Write in simple sentences (grade 8-10 reading level)

Output format: Plain text, no Markdown formatting.

Prompt 5: SEO Meta Title/Description Variants

I need SEO meta fields for [PRODUCT NAME] Shopify listing.

Product: [Brief description]
Primary Keyword: [Keyword phrase]
Price: $XX.XX

Task: Generate 3 variants each for:
1. Meta Title (50-60 characters, include keyword)
2. Meta Description (120-150 characters, benefit-focused)

Constraints:
- Must include primary keyword naturally
- No clickbait or hype language
- Focus on clear value proposition

Output: Numbered list, plain text.

Human Checkpoints (CRITICAL)

Always verify AI outputs before using them:

  • ‚úÖ Angle Accuracy: Do extracted angles match what you saw in ads? (AI sometimes invents patterns)
  • ‚úÖ Brand Voice: Does copy match DriveOn anti-hype tone? (Edit out exaggerations)
  • ‚úÖ Factual Accuracy: Are feature claims real? (AI may hallucinate product capabilities)
  • ‚úÖ Competitor Data: Did AI accurately summarize competitors? (Cross-check 1-2 stores)
  • ‚úÖ No Hype: Remove any fake urgency, exaggerated benefits, or unverifiable claims
‚ö†Ô∏è AI Is a Draft Generator, Not a Decision Maker

AI accelerates drafting but cannot replace judgment. You are responsible for:

  • Verifying all facts and claims
  • Ensuring brand voice compliance (L2-08 guardrails)
  • Making final go/no-go research decisions
  • QA-ing all listing content before launch

If you use AI-generated content verbatim without review, you are violating this SOP.

Tools & Platforms

  • Use Cases: Drafting, summarization, pattern extraction, copy variants
  • Not Recommended: Using AI for final R3 approval decisions (that's Lead Strategist's role)
