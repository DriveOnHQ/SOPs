Role Purpose & Context

Why this role exists within the CaniCol v4.0 lean-volume testing model

Mission

The Creative Director is the strategic and operational leader of Pod 2 (Creative Factory). You are responsible for transforming R3 Product Packets from Pod 1 into test-ready creative systems that maximize the probability of P1/P2 test success while respecting strict economic constraints.

How This Role Drives Hit-Rate Economics

  • Portfolio Thinking: CaniCol v4.0 expects ~4-6% true winners from 45-60 monthly tests. Your creative strategy must balance volume execution with selective quality focus.
  • Budget Discipline: P1 tests run on $40-60 budgets. Your creative packs must deliver performance signals within these tight constraints.
  • Kill Velocity: 70-80% of products fail P1. Your role includes rapid creative triage‚Äîknowing when to iterate vs. when to let a product die.
  • Cross-Pod Integration: You bridge Product Intelligence (Pod 1) insights with Media & Store (Pod 3) execution, ensuring creative coherence across the testing pipeline.

Connection to Holdings & Brand Architecture

Per L0-02, DriveOn Commerce operates as the "dropshipping engine" under DriveOn Holdings. Your creative work must:

  • Respect the anti-hype, systems-first positioning defined in brand canon
  • Follow L2-08 Master Brand Platform guidelines for tone, messaging, and visual identity
  • Maintain quality bar that reflects DriveOn's operational excellence positioning

Workflow 1: R3 ‚Üí P1 Creative Brief ‚ñº

Trigger

R3 packet approval notification in #p2-briefs from Pod 1 Lead Strategist

Inputs

  • R3 Product Packet (Notion + Drive)
  • Creative intel from P1-R0-CREATIVE-INTEL
  • Historical performance data for similar products

Steps

  1. Read R3 Packet (10 min)
    • Primary offer & unique mechanism
    • Target audience & pain points
    • Competitor landscape & differentiation
    • AOV, margins, fulfillment constraints
  2. Review Creative Intel (5 min)
    • Check swipe files for niche/category
    • Note winning hooks & formats
    • Identify saturated vs. fresh angles
  3. Define Testing Strategy (10 min)
    • Select 2-3 primary angles (Problem-Agitate, Transformation, Mechanism Reveal, etc.)
    • Determine format mix: 60% video, 40% static (typical)
    • Plan for 4-6 total P1 variants
  4. Create Creative Brief in Notion (15 min)
    • Database: "Creative Briefs"
    • Fields: Product_ID, Brief_ID, Angles, Hooks, Format_Mix, Key_Benefits, Objections, CTA_Strategy, Brand_Guardrails
    • Link to R3 packet and Drive asset folders
  5. Assign to Team (5 min)
    • Hero Editor: 2-3 video variants
    • Volume Editor: 1-2 cutdowns/formats
    • Static Designer: 1-2 static ads
    • Copy: hooks, scripts, captions
  6. Set Timeline & ClickUp Tasks (5 min)
    • Create ClickUp tasks in "Creative Pipeline" list
    • Due date: 3-5 business days from R3 approval
    • Status: BRIEF_CREATED

Outputs

  • Creative Brief (Notion)
  • ClickUp tasks assigned to team
  • Notification in #p2-briefs confirming brief creation

Quality Checks

  • ‚úì Brief includes 2-3 distinct angles, not just variations
  • ‚úì Strategy considers P1 budget constraints ($40-60)
  • ‚úì Format mix appropriate for niche (video-heavy for demo products, static OK for simple benefits)
  • ‚úì All team members have clear assignments with context
Workflow 2: Creative Review & QA ‚ñº

Trigger

Team member marks creative as "REVIEW_READY" in ClickUp or posts in #p2-edits-hero / #p2-edits-volume

Inputs

  • Completed creative assets (Drive folder)
  • Creative brief & product context (Notion)
  • Brand guidelines (L2-08)

Steps

  1. Access Asset (2 min)
    • Navigate to Drive folder: /DriveOn Commerce/Creative Assets/[Product_ID]/
    • Open video/static in appropriate viewer
    • Have creative brief open side-by-side
  2. Hook & Attention Review (3-5 min)
    • First 3 seconds: Does it stop the scroll?
    • Hook clarity: Is the promise/problem clear?
    • Visual quality: Professional or too "AI-slop"?
  3. Strategic Alignment (3-5 min)
    • Angle: Matches brief? (Problem-Agitate, Transformation, etc.)
    • Key benefits: Communicated clearly?
    • Objections: Addressed or ignored?
    • CTA: Clear and actionable?
  4. Brand & Compliance Check (2-3 min)
    • Tone: Anti-hype, systems-first (per L2-08)?
    • Claims: No unsubstantiated/illegal claims?
    • Visual identity: On-brand colors, fonts, style?
  5. Technical Specs (1-2 min)
    • Format: MP4/MOV for video, PNG/JPG for static?
    • Resolution: Meets platform requirements (1080x1920 for reels, 1200x628 for feed, etc.)?
    • File size: Under limits for upload?
  6. Decision & Feedback (3-5 min)
    • Approve: Move to CREATIVE_APPROVED in ClickUp, add to P1 pack
    • Revise: Provide specific, actionable feedback in ClickUp comment:
      • What's working well
      • 1-3 specific changes needed
      • Revised due date (typically +1 day)
    • Kill: If fundamentally off-brief or unsalvageable, kill and reassign to different specialist

Outputs

  • ClickUp status update: CREATIVE_APPROVED or NEEDS_REVISION
  • Feedback notes in ClickUp (if revisions needed)
  • Approved assets added to P1 Creative Pack tracking

Quality Bar

Aspect Minimum Standard
Hook (first 3s) Clear pattern interrupt or curiosity gap
Angle Execution Matches brief, not generic/off-brief
Brand Alignment Passes L2-08 tone & visual guidelines
Technical Quality Platform-ready specs, no upload blockers
Workflow 3: P1_PASS ‚Üí P2 Variant Strategy ‚ñº

Trigger

Pod 3 Media Buyer marks product as P1_PASS in Notion and posts results to #p3-p2-optimisation

Inputs

  • P1 test results (CTR, CPC, depth events by creative)
  • Original P1 creative brief & pack
  • Budget remaining: ~$40-50 for P2 top-up

Steps

  1. Analyze P1 Performance (10 min)
    • Identify winning creative(s): highest CTR, lowest CPC, most depth events
    • Note which angle/hook/format performed best
    • Check comments/engagement for qualitative insights
  2. Define P2 Strategy (10 min)
    • Option A - Iterate on Winner: Create 2-4 variants of best-performing creative (different hooks, formats, CTAs)
    • Option B - Test Adjacent Angle: Try 1-2 creatives on a different angle if P1 was borderline
    • Option C - Format Expansion: If video won, test static; if static won, test video
  3. Create P2 Brief (10 min)
    • Notion: Update product's Creative Brief with P2 section
    • Specify: 2-4 new variants, clear hypotheses, timeline
    • Assign to team (typically faster turnaround: 1-2 days)
  4. Prioritize P2 Work (5 min)
    • P2 work is HIGHER priority than new P1 work (winner>new test)
    • Ensure team understands urgency‚ÄîPod 3 is waiting to scale
    • Set tight deadline: 1-2 business days from P1_PASS

Outputs

  • P2 creative brief in Notion
  • ClickUp tasks for P2 variants
  • Team notification in #p2-briefs

Quality Checks

  • ‚úì P2 strategy is data-informed (not just "make more ads")
  • ‚úì Clear hypothesis for each variant (testing specific element)
  • ‚úì Timeline respects Pod 3 urgency (winners can't wait)
Workflow 4: Team Capacity & WIP Management ‚ñº

Trigger

Daily (during morning setup) or when approaching WIP limits

Steps

  1. Check Pod-Level WIP (5 min)
    • ClickUp: Count tasks in "BRIEF_CREATED", "IN_PROGRESS", "REVIEW_READY" statuses
    • Your cap: 12-15 products concurrently
    • If at cap, no new briefs until something ships or gets killed
  2. Check Individual Role WIP (5 min)
    • Hero Editor: 8-10 edits max
    • Volume Editors: 10-12 edits max
    • Static Designers: 10-12 assets max
    • Copy: 10-12 products max
  3. Identify Bottlenecks (3 min)
    • Who's overloaded vs. underutilized?
    • Are reviews backing up (your bottleneck)?
    • Any blockers causing delays (asset sourcing, copy approval)?
  4. Rebalance & Prioritize (5 min)
    • Shift work between editors if possible
    • Defer lowest-priority new products
    • Kill stalled products that aren't progressing
    • Escalate resource gaps if structural (need more editors)
  5. Communicate Changes (3 min)
    • Update team in #p2-briefs on priority shifts
    • Update Pod 1 if you're deferring new R3 intakes
    • Flag escalations in #p1-blockers if needed

Outputs

  • Balanced WIP across team
  • Clear priority queue for next 1-3 days
  • Escalations flagged to PM/Founder if needed
‚ö†Ô∏è WIP Management Principles
  • Protect throughput: Better to ship 10 complete packs than have 20 half-done
  • Kill aggressively: Products that stall >5 days with no clear path forward should be killed
  • P2 > P1: Winners always get priority over new tests
  • Escalate early: Don't silently operate over WIP‚Äîflag capacity issues

Escalation & Decision Rights

What you can decide alone vs. when to escalate

Decisions You Make Alone

  • Creative angle selection for P1 tests
  • Format mix (video vs. static) within 4-6 asset range
  • Assignment of briefs to team specialists
  • Approval/revision of individual creative assets
  • P2 variant strategy for P1_PASS products
  • Daily/weekly priority sequencing within WIP
  • Team feedback & coaching

Decisions Requiring PM / Founder

  • WIP Cap Exceptions: Going above 15 products concurrently
  • Budget Exceptions: Requesting >6 creatives for P1 (outside canon)
  • Resource Gaps: Need to hire additional editor/designer
  • Strategic Shifts: Major changes to creative philosophy or pod structure
  • Brand Guideline Questions: Uncertain if something violates L2-08
  • Kill vs. Salvage: Uncertain whether to kill stalled product or try creative pivot

Escalation Procedures

For Blockers (Operational)

  1. Post in #p1-blockers (if cross-pod) or #p4-systems-ops (if infrastructure)
  2. Include:
    • What's blocked
    • Impact (products affected, timeline at risk)
    • What you've tried
    • Decision needed or help requested
  3. Tag PM or relevant role lead
  4. Expected response time: 4 business hours

For Canon Questions (Strategic)

  1. Check L2-08 (Brand Platform) and L2-05 (Testing Canon) first
  2. If unclear, post in #sys-canon-updates with question
  3. Tag Founder / Integrator
  4. Expected response time: 1 business day

For Performance Issues (Quality/Team)

  1. Document specific issue with examples
  2. Attempt direct feedback first (1:1 with team member)
  3. If persistent, escalate to Founder with:
    • Pattern description
    • Impact on deliverables
    • Recommended action
  4. Channel: Direct message or #p4-pm-daily-standup if appropriate

Escalate early, not late. Key triggers:

  • WIP at 15+ for >2 days with no clear path to clear
  • Any product stalled >5 business days in creative
  • Team member consistently missing quality bar after feedback
  • Resource constraint impacting ability to support 45-60 test pipeline
