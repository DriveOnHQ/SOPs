Role Purpose & Context

Why this role exists in the CaniCol v4.0 model and how it supports the 4-6% winner hit rate

Why This Role Exists

The Intel Researcher (R1/R2) is the workhorse of DriveOn's Product Intelligence pod. You sit at the critical middle stage of the research pipeline, transforming rough R0 signals into validated R3 packets ready for paid testing.

Your work directly protects DriveOn's $4,000-$5,500 monthly test bank by ensuring that only products with genuine market viability, clear angles, and manageable risk profiles advance to P1 tests ($40-$60 each).

Connection to CaniCol Economics

DriveOn tests 45-60 products/month expecting 4-6% winners (2-4 products). This lean-volume model only works if:

  • R0/R1 filters kill obviously bad ideas early
  • R2 deep research (YOUR WORK) dramatically reduces false positives
  • R3 packets entering P1 have genuine signal, not just hype
  • Kill discipline at every gate maintains portfolio quality

If R2 passes weak products to R3, the entire testing system wastes budget on predictable failures. Your gate is where market reality meets product hypothesis.

How You Support the Hit Rate

Winners aren't found by testing more volume‚Äîthey're found by better filtering. Your R2 dossiers provide:

  • Angle discovery: What hooks actually convert, not just what looks cool
  • Risk assessment: Quality complaints, logistics nightmares, policy issues
  • Competitor intelligence: Price bands, creative patterns, proven demand
  • Supplier validation: Can we actually source and fulfill this profitably?
‚öñÔ∏è Canon Connection

This role directly implements L1-01 CaniCol Model v4.0 lean-volume philosophy: "Kill fast, test smart, scale winners." Your R2 research is the point where we validate hypotheses before spending real money.

Non-negotiable rules from L0-L3 canon that govern this role

Economics & Volume (from L1-01 + L2-05)

  • Testing Volume: ~45-60 products/month reach P1/P2
  • Your Contribution: 3-4 R3-ready packets/week per 1.0 FTE researcher
  • Pipeline Math: Typically 3-5√ó R1 volume ‚Üí many killed at R1/R2
  • P1 Budget: $40-$60 per product (your research protects this spend)
  • Hit Rate: 4-6% true winners (2-4/month from 45-60 tests)

WIP Limits (from L2-03)

Per 1.0 FTE, you must never exceed:

  • R1 Active: ‚â§6 packets
  • R2 Active: ‚â§3 packets (deep work requires focus)
  • R3-Prep: ‚â§1 packet (formatting for Lead approval)
‚ö†Ô∏è WIP Discipline

These caps follow Kanban deep-work norms: you should rarely exceed 2-3 heavy R2 items at once. WIP violations = signal for process review, not automatic WIP inflation. If you're consistently blocked, escalate to R3 Lead or PM.

Gate Definitions (from L2-05)

R1 - Plausibility Check:

  • Clear demand or trend signal exists
  • No obvious red flags (scam, policy risk, impossible logistics)
  • Output: "No obvious reason to reject" vs "hard no"
  • Time: Quick filters, 15-30 min per product

R2 - Deep Research & Angle Discovery:

  • Competitor mapping, price bands, review analysis
  • Risk checks (quality complaints, returns, platform policy)
  • Angle discovery: what hooks actually convert?
  • Supplier shortlist & basic landed cost modeling
  • Time: 1-2 working days per product (spread across researchers)

Research Cycle Time

From L2-02: Most R2 candidates should be resolved within 1-2 working days per product. This follows lean-startup "build-measure-learn" loops where the "build" is the research packet and the "measure" is the P1 result.

‚öñÔ∏è Authority & Approval

You own R1 and R2 decisions but R3 approval belongs to P1-R3-LEAD-STRATEGIST. You prepare packets; Lead decides final Go/No-Go and prioritization for P1 tests.

Core Workflows

Step-by-step workflows for primary research operations

Workflow 1: R1 Plausibility Check

‚ñº

Trigger: R0 Scout or R1 Scout-Sourcing moves product to R1 Shortlist in ClickUp

Inputs:

  • ClickUp Task in R1 Shortlist list, status = R1_READY
  • Notion Product OS page (basic fields populated)
  • Initial product links (TikTok, Amazon, AliExpress, etc.)

Steps:

  1. Claim the task: Assign yourself in ClickUp, move status to R1_IN_PROGRESS
  2. Quick demand check (5 min):
    • TikTok: Search product name + category, check view counts and engagement
    • Amazon: Check BSR rank, review count, rating (aim for 4.0+, 500+ reviews)
    • Google Trends: Validate search interest hasn't crashed
  3. Red flag scan (5 min):
    • Review 1-star Amazon reviews: Quality complaints? Frequent returns?
    • Policy check: Prohibited category? Health claims? Age restrictions?
    • Logistics: Oversized/fragile? Batteries? Liquids?
  4. Market plausibility (5 min):
    • Competitor check: Are other dropshippers actively running this?
    • Price band: Can we price at $29-$79 sweet spot with margin?
    • Trend context: Early/peak/declining? Seasonal?
  5. Decision:
    • PASS to R2: Move to R2 Dossiers list, status R2_READY
    • KILL: Move status to R1_FAIL ‚Üí KILLED, add 2-3 sentence kill note
  6. Update Notion Product OS: Set Current Gate to R2 or add kill reason
  7. Log time: Track 15-30 min in ClickUp time tracking

Outputs:

  • ClickUp task moved to R2 or killed
  • Notion Product OS updated with gate status
  • Brief notes on why passed or failed
‚ö†Ô∏è R1 Kill Discipline

R1 is a filter, not deep validation. If there's any obvious red flag, kill it. Don't waste R2 time on products with structural problems. Better to kill 70-80% at R1 and focus R2 on genuine contenders.

Workflow 2: R2 Deep Research & Angle Discovery

‚ñº

Trigger: Product passes R1 and lands in R2 Dossiers list

Inputs:

  • ClickUp task in R2 Dossiers list, status = R2_READY
  • Notion Product OS page with R1 notes
  • Product links and initial intel

Steps:

  1. Claim and start (10 min):
    • Assign yourself in ClickUp, move status to R2_IN_PROGRESS
    • Create Drive folder: /DriveOn Commerce/Product Research/R2_Deep_Research/PRD-YYYYMMDD-XXX/
    • Open Notion Product OS page for live note-taking
  2. Competitor deep dive (30-45 min):
    • Identify 3-5 top competitors running this product (Minea, FB Ad Library)
    • Screenshot best-performing ads, save to Drive folder
    • Analyze creative angles: Problem-solution? Transformation? Social proof? Demo?
    • Note price points: What are competitors charging? What's converting?
    • Check store quality: Are these validation stores or brand builds?
  3. Review mining & objections (20-30 min):
    • Amazon 1-star, 2-star, 3-star reviews: Common complaints?
    • TikTok comment sections: What questions do buyers ask?
    • Reddit/forums: Real user experiences, disappointments?
    • Document top 3-5 objections that creative/copy must address
  4. Angle discovery & hook validation (30 min):
    • Identify 2-3 primary angles that resonate with real buyers
    • Find proof points: Before/after results, testimonials, demo videos
    • Validate hooks against actual engagement (likes, comments, shares)
    • Prioritize: Which angle has most signal? Which is easiest to execute?
  5. Risk & compliance check (15-20 min):
    • Quality concerns: Are returns/complaints systemic or isolated?
    • Policy risks: Any health claims, copyright issues, age restrictions?
    • Logistics: Shipping times realistic? Size/weight manageable?
    • Rate risk level: LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH with justification
  6. Supplier & economics validation (20-30 min):
    • Validate supplier options from R1 Scout-Sourcing
    • Check MOQ, lead time, shipping options
    • Rough unit economics: Cost + shipping + fees vs target AOV
    • Can we hit 40-50% margin at competitive price? If not, flag.
  7. Build R2 dossier in Notion (20-30 min):
    • Product Overview: Name, category, target AOV, brief description
    • Market Context: Trend status, competitor landscape, price bands
    • Angle Strategy: 2-3 primary angles with hooks and proof points
    • Risk Assessment: Quality, policy, logistics concerns with severity rating
    • Creative Intel: Top competitor ad examples, engagement patterns
    • Objection Map: Top 3-5 buyer concerns and how to address
    • Supplier Notes: Shortlist, pricing, lead times, constraints
    • Recommendation: GO / NO-GO / BORDERLINE with reasoning
  8. Update ClickUp & handoff (10 min):
    • Move status from R2_IN_PROGRESS ‚Üí R2_REVIEW ‚Üí R3_READY
    • Add comment with link to Notion dossier and Drive folder
    • Tag R3 Lead Strategist for review
    • Post handoff in Discord #p1-r3-approvals with format below
  9. Log time: Track 2-4 hours total in ClickUp (spread across 1-2 days)

Outputs:

  • Complete R2 dossier in Notion Product OS
  • Drive folder with competitor screenshots, research artifacts
  • ClickUp task moved to R3_READY for Lead approval
  • Discord handoff notification
üí° Angle Discovery Pro Tips
  • Don't guess angles‚Äîlook for what's actually converting in ads and comments
  • Prioritize angles that are defensible with proof (not just hype)
  • The best angle is often hidden in 3-star reviews (disappointed but specific)
  • If no clear angle emerges after 30 min, that's a signal‚Äîconsider killing

Workflow 3: R2 Kill Decision

‚ñº

Trigger: During R2 research, you discover disqualifying factors

Kill Criteria (any one is sufficient):

  • No clear angle: After 30-45 min of competitor analysis, no compelling hook emerges
  • Systemic quality issues: Consistent complaints across multiple suppliers/platforms
  • Policy violations: Health claims, copyright, age restrictions impossible to navigate
  • Economics don't work: Can't hit 40% margin at competitive price
  • Saturated market: 10+ competitors with identical positioning, no differentiation possible
  • Trend collapse: Google Trends shows sharp decline, no recovery signal

Steps:

  1. Document kill reason: Add 3-5 sentence note in Notion Product OS explaining why
  2. Update ClickUp: Move status to R2_FAIL ‚Üí KILLED
  3. Add kill tag: Use custom field or label (e.g., "No Angle", "Quality Risk", "Economics")
  4. Brief Discord notification: Post in #p1-r2-dossiers: "PRD-YYYYMMDD-XXX killed at R2: [reason]"
  5. Archive Drive folder: Move to /R2_Deep_Research/KILLED/ for later reference
  6. Log time: Track partial time spent before kill decision
üí° Kill Fast, Move On

Don't sink 3-4 hours into a borderline product hoping it gets better. If you're 30-45 min into R2 and no clear angle emerges, that's a kill signal. Protect your WIP for genuine contenders.

Quality Standards: Good vs Bad R2 Dossiers

‚úÖ Good R2 Dossier Example
  • Angle Strategy: 2-3 specific angles backed by competitor ad examples and engagement data
  • Proof Points: Clear before/after results, testimonials, demo videos with links
  • Risk Assessment: Honest evaluation with severity rating and mitigation notes
  • Objection Map: Top 3-5 buyer concerns extracted from real reviews, not guessed
  • Competitor Intel: Screenshots of 3-5 active ads with performance context (likes, comments)
  • Supplier Validation: Multiple options with pricing, MOQ, lead time verified
  • Recommendation: Clear GO/NO-GO with 2-3 sentence business reasoning
‚ùå Bad R2 Dossier Example
  • Vague Angles: "People want better sleep" (no hook, no proof, no differentiation)
  • Invented Objections: "Customers might worry about price" (not from real data)
  • Missing Risk Context: "Some quality concerns" (no severity, no mitigation)
  • Generic Competitor Notes: "Lots of people selling this" (no specific intel)
  • Unverified Suppliers: "Found on AliExpress" (no pricing, MOQ, or lead time)
  • Weak Recommendation: "Might work, not sure" (no clear decision)

AI Usage & Prompt Patterns

Ready-to-copy AI prompts for common research tasks (use responsibly with human checkpoints)

When to Use AI in R1/R2 Research

  • Review mining & summarization: Extract themes from 50+ Amazon reviews
  • Competitor ad analysis: Pattern recognition across multiple ad creatives
  • Objection mapping: Cluster buyer concerns from comments and reviews
  • Angle brainstorming: Generate variations on validated hooks (not from scratch)

DO NOT use AI for:

  • Making GO/NO-GO decisions (that's your judgment)
  • Inventing product angles without real competitive data
  • Guessing supplier economics or logistics constraints
  • Writing final dossier recommendations (you own this)

Prompt 1: Amazon Review Mining & Objection Extraction

‚ñº 
I'm researching a dropshipping product: [PRODUCT NAME].

I've gathered 50+ Amazon reviews across multiple listings. I need you to:

1. Identify the TOP 5 customer objections or pain points mentioned repeatedly
2. For each objection, provide:
   - The specific concern (quote examples if helpful)
   - How frequently it appears (rough estimate)
   - Severity level (minor annoyance vs deal-breaker)
   - Suggested mitigation for ad copy or product positioning

Format as a numbered list. Focus on REAL customer concerns, not hypothetical ones.

Reviews: [PASTE REVIEWS HERE]

Human Checkpoint: Verify top objections against your own review reading. AI may miss context or overweight rare complaints.

Prompt 2: Competitor Ad Angle Analysis

‚ñº 
I'm analyzing competitor ads for: [PRODUCT NAME]

I have 5-10 ads from successful dropshippers (screenshots + transcripts). I need you to:

1. Identify the primary angle/hook used in each ad
2. Categorize angles (e.g., Problem-Solution, Transformation, Social Proof, Demo, Urgency)
3. Rank angles by apparent effectiveness (based on engagement, ad longevity, copy quality)
4. Highlight specific hooks or phrases that seem to resonate

Format as a table:
| Ad # | Angle Category | Hook/Opening Line | Engagement Signal | Notes |

Ads: [PASTE AD TRANSCRIPTS OR DESCRIPTIONS HERE]

Human Checkpoint: Cross-reference AI's angle categories with your own understanding. Verify engagement signals against actual TikTok/FB metrics.

Prompt 3: Angle Variation Generation (from Validated Hook)

‚ñº 
I have a validated product angle for: [PRODUCT NAME]

Validated Angle: [e.g., "Sleep quality transformation using doctor-backed science"]
Supporting Data: [e.g., "3 competitors running this angle, 50k+ likes, comments asking 'does it work?'"]

Generate 5 variations of this angle that:
1. Maintain the core promise (sleep quality + credibility)
2. Adjust the framing or entry point (e.g., pain point vs aspiration)
3. Could be tested as secondary angles in P1/P2

Format as a numbered list with brief rationale for each variation.

DO NOT invent new angles from scratch‚Äîonly variations on the validated core.

Human Checkpoint: Evaluate variations against your R2 research. Do they still align with buyer objections and competitor patterns?

Prompt 4: Risk Assessment Summary

‚ñº 
I'm completing a risk assessment for: [PRODUCT NAME]

Context:
- Category: [e.g., Health & Wellness]
- Price Point: $[XX]
- Target AOV: $[XX]
- Supplier: [e.g., AliExpress, verified supplier]

Research Findings:
[PASTE YOUR RISK NOTES: quality complaints, policy concerns, logistics issues, etc.]

I need you to:
1. Categorize risks into: Quality, Policy/Compliance, Logistics, Market Saturation
2. Rate each risk: LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH
3. Suggest mitigation strategies for MEDIUM/HIGH risks
4. Provide a final risk verdict: GO / NO-GO / BORDERLINE

Format as a structured risk matrix with clear severity ratings.

Human Checkpoint: YOU make the final GO/NO-GO decision, not AI. Use AI output as a structured thinking aid, then apply your judgment.

Escalation & Decision Rights

When you decide alone vs when to escalate to R3 Lead or PM

You Decide Alone:

  • R1 Pass/Kill: You own plausibility check‚Äîno approval needed
  • R2 Kill: If clear disqualifying factors emerge, kill and document
  • Research methods: Which tools to use, how deep to dig, time allocation
  • Dossier structure: How to organize findings (as long as it's clear)
  • Angle prioritization: Which angles to highlight first in R2 dossier

Escalate to R3 Lead Strategist:

  • Borderline GO/NO-GO: Strong angle but high risk, or weak angle but strategic fit
  • Strategic questions: "Should we prioritize this niche over others?"
  • Resource conflicts: "Scout keeps pushing products I'm killing at R2"
  • R3 rejections: If Lead rejects multiple dossiers, ask for calibration
  • Expected response time: Within 24 hours for blockers, 48 hours for calibration

Escalate to Pod PM / Ops:
