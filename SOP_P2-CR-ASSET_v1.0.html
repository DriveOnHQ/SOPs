Role Overview

When You Should Use This SOP

You are P2-CR-ASSET-XX if your daily work is:

  • Sourcing product footage, B-roll, lifestyle clips, and stock assets for 8-10 products in parallel
  • Organizing raw materials into clean, editor-ready asset packs
  • Coordinating with Creative Director and editors to ensure material matches brief requirements
  • Maintaining licensing documentation and avoiding copyright issues
  • Delivering 10-20 complete asset packs per week to support P1/P2 creative production
üéØ Core Responsibility

You are responsible (R) for sourcing and organizing raw creative materials. You are not editing videos or deciding creative strategy ‚Äì that's the editors and Creative Director. Your job is to find, organize, and deliver the raw ingredients that make their work possible.

Role Purpose & Context

üé¨

Why This Role Exists

DriveOn Commerce tests 45-60 products/month through P1/P2. Each product needs 4-6 P1 creatives, totaling 250-400 assets/month across the Creative Factory.

The bottleneck: Editors can't source, organize, and edit at CaniCol velocity. When one person tries to do both, throughput collapses or quality suffers.

Your role solves this: By specializing in sourcing and organizing raw materials, you let editors focus 100% on editing. This division of labor is standard in high-performing creative teams and is how modern performance marketing teams hit 250+ assets/month with quality.

How You Support CaniCol Economics

CaniCol v4.0 relies on:

  • P1 Budget: $40-$60 per product
  • P1+P2 Total: $70-$120 (typically $80-$100)
  • Hit Rate: ~4-6% winners (~2-4 products/month)
  • Test Bank: ~$4,000-$5,500/month for P1+P2

Your sourcing speed and quality directly impact whether editors can hit 4-6 P1 creatives per product on time and on budget. Delays in asset delivery cascade into missed test windows and wasted budget.

Canon Constraints

‚ö†Ô∏è Non-Negotiable Rules

These constraints come from L0-L3 canon and cannot be changed at the L4 (implementation) level. If reality conflicts with these, escalate to Creative Director or PM.

Economic Constraints (L1-01, L2-05)

  • Testing Volume: 45-60 products/month in P1/P2
  • Creative Target: 4-6 P1 creatives per product baseline
  • Pod Capacity: 250-400 total assets/month (P2 pod-wide)
  • No Budget Changes: You cannot increase P1 or P2 budgets to "buy better assets"

WIP & Throughput Constraints (L2-03, L2-07)

  • Your WIP Cap: 8-10 products in asset-sourcing in parallel (per 1.0 FTE)
  • Your Throughput: 10-20 ready-to-use asset packs/week
  • Editor WIP Caps: Hero editors: 4-6 products / 6-8 edits; Volume editors: 8-10 edits
  • Do Not Exceed WIP: If you're at 10 products and a new one arrives, it waits in backlog or you escalate

Pass/Kill Criteria & Testing Canon (L2-05)

You do not make pass/kill decisions, but understanding them helps you prioritize:

  • P1 Pass Sketch: CTR, CPC, depth event thresholds (editors and buyers decide, not you)
  • P1 Fail = No P2 Assets: If a product fails P1, there's no need for P2 variants (usually)
  • P2 Pass ‚Üí P3: Winners get scaled; you may need to source additional refresh assets later

Approved Tool Stack (L2/L3)

You may use:

  • ClickUp: Task tracking, asset request statuses
  • Notion: Product OS, creative briefs, asset library index
  • Google Drive: File storage and delivery
  • Discord: Handoff notifications and quick questions
  • Sourcing Platforms: Pexels, Pixabay, Envato (via team account), supplier content (via approved channels)

You may NOT:

  • Introduce new paid tools without approval (e.g., expensive stock libraries not in budget)
  • Use unapproved file-sharing services (Dropbox, WeTransfer, etc.)
  • Store assets outside Drive or ClickUp-linked locations
üö´ No UGC Creator / Self-Filming Unless Canonized

As of v4.0 canon, there is no approved UGC creator network or self-filming content strategy. All sourcing is via supplier content, stock platforms, or existing web-available footage.

If a Creative Director or PM asks for UGC-style content, that means: find existing clips that look like UGC (user reviews, unboxings, lifestyle use), not commission new creators. If this changes, canon will be updated and you'll be notified.

Actions:

  4. If brief is unclear or missing key info: post in #p2-briefs: "@CreativeDirector ‚Äì brief for PRD-X needs clarification on [specific question]". Do not start sourcing until clarified.

Source Product Footage

Actions:

  1. Check supplier content first:

  2. Emotion & Relatability: Find clips showing:

    • Use cases (people using similar products in real contexts)

Qualitative Standards

Good Asset Pack Example

  • Variety: 10-15 product clips showing different angles, uses, contexts
  • Hook Alignment: B-roll clips directly support each hook in the brief (e.g., "morning routine" hook ‚Üí 5+ morning-themed clips)
  • Quality: All clips 1080p+, clean framing, no watermarks (except licensed stock watermarks that editors can crop)
  • Organization: Subfolders make sense (Product_Footage / Lifestyle_Broll), file names are clear
  • Licensing: Every clip documented, sources noted, no red flags
  • Editor Feedback: Editor says "Great pack, easy to work with" or no revision requests

Poor Asset Pack Example

  • Too Few Clips: Only 5-6 total clips (not enough variety for editors)
  • Off-Brief: Clips don't match hooks (e.g., brief says "home use", all clips are outdoor/travel)
  • Low Quality: 720p or blurry clips, heavy watermarks, bad framing
  • Messy Organization: All files dumped in one folder with cryptic names like "video1.mp4", "clip.mp4"
  • No Licensing: No _LICENSING_NOTES.txt file, editors don't know if clips are safe to use
  • Editor Feedback: "Can't use most of these, need to re-source" (revision required)

Use Case: You have a creative brief with abstract hooks like "Feel confident anywhere" and need to translate this into search terms for Pexels/Pixabay.

Prompt Pattern:

I'm sourcing B-roll clips for an ecommerce ad. The product is [product name/category], and the creative hook is "[hook from brief]". Generate 10 search keywords I can use on stock video platforms like Pexels and Pixabay to find relevant lifestyle and B-roll clips. Focus on emotions, settings, and actions that align with the hook.

Example Output:

Hook: "Perfect for busy mornings"
Keywords: morning routine, coffee brewing, rushing to work, waking up, breakfast prep, getting ready, alarm clock, sunrise, stressed morning, organized morning

Human Checkpoint: You review the keywords and pick the 3-5 most relevant before searching. Don't blindly use all 10.

2. File Organization Suggestions

Use Case: You have 25 clips for a complex product with multiple hooks and want to organize them into logical subfolders.

Prompt Pattern:

I have 25 video clips for a product. The creative brief has these hooks: [Hook 1], [Hook 2], [Hook 3]. I want to organize the clips into subfolders. Suggest a folder structure that makes it easy for video editors to find clips for each hook.

Example Output:

Suggested structure:
- Product_Footage/ (all product demos)
- Lifestyle_Broll/
  - Hook_1_Morning_Routine/
  - Hook_2_Travel/
  - Hook_3_Home_Organization/
- Generic_Broll/ (clips that work for multiple hooks)

Human Checkpoint: You adapt the structure to fit your actual clips. If a hook has only 2-3 clips, you may not need a separate subfolder.

3. Licensing Quick-Check (with caution)

Use Case: You found a clip on a platform you're less familiar with (e.g., Envato) and want to understand the license terms quickly.

Prompt Pattern:

I'm sourcing clips for an ecommerce ad that will run on Facebook/TikTok. I found a clip on [platform] with this license: "[paste license text]". Can I use this for paid ads? What are the key restrictions?

Human Checkpoint: ALWAYS verify AI's answer against the platform's actual license page. Do not rely solely on AI for licensing decisions. If unsure, escalate to Creative Director or PM.

4. Batch File Renaming Scripts (Advanced)

Use Case: You have 30 clips with default names like "pexels-12345.mp4" and want to rename them to match DriveOn conventions.

Prompt Pattern:

Write a bash script that renames all .mp4 files in a folder to the format: PRD-YYYYMMDD-XXX_[Type]_[Number].mp4. The script should:
1. Ask me for the Product ID (e.g., PRD-20251208-042)
2. Ask me for the Type (e.g., "Product" or "Broll")
3. Rename files sequentially (01, 02, 03, etc.)

Human Checkpoint: Test the script on a copy of the files first, not the originals. Only use if you're comfortable with command-line tools.

ü§ñ AI Limitations & Risks
  • AI cannot verify licenses: It may misinterpret terms. Always check platform docs yourself.
  • AI search suggestions may be generic: Use them as a starting point, refine based on actual brief.
  • AI cannot assess creative fit: Only you (and editors) can judge if a clip matches the hook/angle.

Escalation & Decision Rights

Decisions You Can Make Alone

  • Which clips to download: As long as they're on-brief and properly licensed
  • File organization: Subfolder structure within your asset packs (as long as you follow DriveOn conventions)
  • Search keywords: Which terms to use on stock platforms
  • Minor revisions: If editor asks for "2-3 more B-roll clips of X", just source and deliver

Decisions Requiring Creative Director or PM

  • Priority conflicts: If you're at WIP cap and two urgent requests arrive simultaneously
  • Budget for paid assets: If free stock isn't cutting it and you want to buy premium clips (Creative Director decides if budget allows)
  • Major revisions: If editor says "This whole pack is off-brief, need to start over" (Creative Director reviews brief and decides next steps)
  • New sourcing strategies: If you want to try a new platform or approach (PM/Creative Director approves before implementing)

When to Escalate to Founder / Canon Owner

  • Chronic WIP overload: If you're consistently at 10+ products for 2+ weeks and new requests keep piling up (signals need for hiring or process change)
  • Canon conflicts: If you encounter a situation where canon rules (WIP caps, throughput targets) are impossible to meet due to external factors (escalate via PM ‚Üí Founder)
  • Major licensing risks: If you discover DriveOn has been using clips with unclear/violated licensing (escalate to Creative Director ‚Üí PM ‚Üí Founder)
