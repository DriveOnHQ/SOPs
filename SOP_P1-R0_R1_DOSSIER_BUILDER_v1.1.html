Role Purpose & Context

Why this role exists in the CaniCol v4.0 lean-volume testing model and how it protects the economics of the entire pipeline.

Mission Statement

Transform noisy market signals into high-quality, supplier-validated R1 Product Packets that protect the test bank by killing weak ideas early and feeding only plausible products into deep research (R2).

How This Role Supports CaniCol Economics

The CaniCol model tests 45-60 products/month at tight budgets ($80-100 P1+P2 typical spend). With only a 4-6% hit rate, we expect most products to fail. Your job is to ensure failures happen at R0/R1 (zero cost) rather than at P1 ($40-60 wasted per product).

Economic Impact:

  • Every bad product killed at R1 saves $80-100 in test spend
  • Fast R0‚ÜíR1 throughput (30-60 min/product) keeps the pipeline full without bottlenecks
  • Supplier validation at R1 prevents "great idea, terrible logistics" disasters at P1
  • Your throughput (20-40 R1 packets/week) directly feeds the 45-60 tests/month target

Position in the Pod System

Pod 1 (Product Intelligence) runs a four-stage research pipeline: R0 ‚Üí R1 ‚Üí R2 ‚Üí R3. You own the front gates (R0, R1), which means:

  • R0: Capture raw product signals from spy tools, ad libraries, trends, marketplaces
  • R1: Quick plausibility filter ‚Äî supplier check, obvious red flags, basic demand signal
  • Handoff: Pass validated R1 packets to R2 researchers for deep dossiers

You are the first line of defense against wasting the team's time and money on unviable products.

Success Pattern

High-performing R0‚ÜíR1 builders develop strong "spidey sense" for what won't work. You're not looking for perfection ‚Äî you're looking for "no obvious reason to reject" so R2 can do deep work on products worth the time.

    • ‚úÖ R1 Candidate: No obvious red flags ‚Üí Set status to R0_TO_R1, move to next workflow

Workflow 2: R1 Plausibility Check & Supplier Validation

‚ñº

Trigger: Task status = R0_TO_R1 in ClickUp R0 Ideas list

Inputs: Product ID, source URL, initial signal notes

Outputs: R1_APPROVED packet in Notion OR R1_FAIL with documented reason

Time Budget: 30-60 minutes per product

Steps:
  • Step 1: Move task from R0 Ideas to R1 Shortlist in ClickUp, set status to R1_IN_PROGRESS
  • Step 2: Create Notion page in Products OS with Product ID as title, set Gate = R1
  • Step 3: Run basic market scan (10-15 minutes):
    • Check 3-5 competitor listings (Amazon, eBay, niche stores)
    • Note price ranges ($X-$Y typical)
    • Scan reviews for common complaints
    • Check if actively advertised (Minea, FB Ads Library)
  • Step 4: Supplier validation (15-20 minutes):
    • Search AliExpress, Alibaba, CJDropshipping for product
    • Find 2-3 viable suppliers with ratings ‚â• 4.5/5
    • Document: Unit cost, MOQ, lead time, shipping options
    • Calculate rough landed cost (unit + shipping estimate)
  • Step 5: R1 Pass/Fail Decision:
    • ‚ùå R1_FAIL if: No viable suppliers, logistics nightmare, too competitive, policy concerns ‚Üí Document reason in ClickUp comment, close task
    • ‚úÖ R1_PASS if: Suppliers exist, no obvious blockers, basic demand signal ‚Üí Continue to Step 6
  • Step 6: Complete R1 Packet in Notion:
    • Product Overview (2-3 sentences)
    • Target Avatar (who buys this)
    • Price Range ($X-$Y competitive)
    • Supplier Options (table with 2-3 suppliers)
    • Red Flags (any concerns for R2 team)
    • Initial Angles (1-2 hook ideas)
  • Step 7: Update ClickUp:
    • Set status to R1_APPROVED
    • Link Notion page URL in task description
    • Assign to next R2 researcher (use round-robin or PM direction)
  • Step 8: Post handoff notification in Discord #p1-r1-shortlist:

Workflow 3: R1 Kill Documentation & Learning

‚ñº

Trigger: Product fails R1 plausibility check

Purpose: Document why we killed products to improve future R0 filtering and build team knowledge

Steps:
  • Step 1: In ClickUp task, add comment with kill reason using standardized tags:
    • [SUPPLIER] - No viable suppliers or terrible logistics
    • [POLICY] - Platform policy risk
    • [COMPETITIVE] - Market too saturated
    • [DEMAND] - No clear demand signal
    • [ECONOMICS] - Unit economics don't work
  • Step 2: Set task status to R1_FAIL and close task
  • Step 3: Log kill in Notion Products OS (set Status = Killed, Gate = R1, Kill Reason = [tag])
  • Step 4: If pattern emerges (e.g., "all pet products have supplier issues"), flag to PM in Discord #p1-blockers

Learning from Kills

Every kill teaches you what NOT to waste time on at R0. If you kill 5 products for the same reason in one week, update your R0 triage checklist to catch that pattern earlier.

  • Step 4: If viable, create R1 packet (can skip deep market scan since parent already validated demand)
  • Step 5: Fast-track to R1_APPROVED, notify P3 requester in Discord with ETA for R2 completion

Escalation Protocol

When to Escalate

‚ñº

Escalate immediately when you encounter:

  • Capacity Issues: WIP exceeds caps for 2+ days, can't hit throughput targets
  • Quality Disputes: R2 team rejects ‚â•3 of your packets in one week
  • Tool Access Issues: Can't access required tools/systems
  • Policy Gray Areas: Product that might violate policy but unclear
  • Supplier Problems: All suppliers for high-priority product have fatal flaws
  • Workflow Blockers: Upstream or downstream handoff broken

How to Escalate

‚ñº
  1. Discord First: Post in #p1-blockers with tag [ESCALATION]
  2. Include Context:
    • Product ID (if applicable)
    • What you tried
    • What the blocker is
    • Impact on throughput/timeline
  3. Tag Appropriately:
    • @PM for capacity/workflow issues
    • @R2-Lead for quality disputes
    • @Operations for tool/access issues
  4. Follow Up: If no response in 4 hours, DM the relevant person
