Role Purpose & Context

Why this role exists in the CaniCol v4.0 model and how it connects to DriveOn's economics and hit-rate goals.

Mission Statement

The Copywriter & Concept Writer transforms R3 product intelligence into high-performing copy systems that give Media Buyers the best chance of hitting P1 pass thresholds (CTR ‚â•1.3%, CPC ‚â§$1.20) within the $40-60 P1 budget.

Strategic Context

DriveOn Commerce operates a lean-volume dropshipping model:

  • ~45-60 products tested monthly through P1/P2 gates
  • Target 4-6% winner rate (2-4 winners/month)
  • 70-80% killed in P1 within 24-48 hours
  • Portfolio-first mindset over individual product attachment

Your copy directly impacts:

  • Top-of-funnel attention: Hook quality determines scroll-stop rate and CTR
  • Early intent signals: Script clarity drives ATC/IC depth events
  • Testing velocity: Fast, on-brief copy keeps 45-60 products/month flowing
  • Economic discipline: Every word must justify itself within tight P1/P2 budgets

Why This Role Matters

Research from Meta and TikTok shows that hook quality (first 3 seconds) accounts for 60-80% of ad performance variance. In a lean-volume model where most products fail P1, your copy is the first and often only line of defense against wasted spend. A weak hook kills a good product; a strong hook gives a mediocre product a fighting chance.

How You Connect to Holdings & Pods

  • L0 (Holdings): Support DriveOn Commerce's brand positioning as a lean, data-driven engine (not a hype machine)
  • L1 (Model): Enable CaniCol's 3-gate testing by providing 4-6 P1 copy variants and 2-4 P2 iterations per product
  • P1 (Intel): Consume R3 packets with problem/objection intel and creative swipes
  • P2 (Creative): Feed editors with scripts; receive direction from Creative Director
  • P3 (Media): Enable Media Buyers to run valid tests with performance-ready copy

Core Workflows

Step-by-step processes for the most critical copy production tasks. Each workflow includes trigger, inputs, actions, outputs, and quality checks.
Workflow 1: P1 Copy Brief Creation (from R3 Packet)

Trigger

Creative Director assigns product to you OR R3 packet appears in ClickUp "Copy_Queue" with status "Copy_Queue"

Inputs

  • R3 Product Packet (Notion link from ClickUp task)
  • P1-R0-PROBLEM-INTEL outputs (objection banks, pain points)
  • P1-R0-CREATIVE-INTEL swipe files (winning hooks from competitors)
  • Creative Director brief notes (angle guidance, if any)

Actions

  1. Review R3 Packet (10 min):
    • Read problem/objection summary
    • Note AOV range, niche, target demo
    • Identify 2-3 primary angles (problem-solution, authority, social proof, urgency)
  2. Generate Hook Variants (20 min):
    • Use AI prompt (see AI Usage section) to generate 10-15 raw hooks
    • Manually refine to 4-6 best variants
    • Ensure diversity: 2 problem-agitate, 2 curiosity-gap, 1-2 authority/social
  3. Draft Scripts (25 min):
    • Create 2-3 script templates (15-30s, 30-60s)
    • Structure: Hook ‚Üí Problem Agitate ‚Üí Solution Intro ‚Üí Benefit Stack ‚Üí CTA
    • Keep language simple, direct, benefit-focused
  4. Write CTAs & Captions (10 min):
    • 3-5 CTA variations (Shop Now, Learn More, Get Yours, Limited Stock)
    • TikTok/IG captions (2-3 variants, emoji-light, hashtag strategy)
  5. Populate Notion Creative Brief (5 min):
    • Add all copy assets to Creative Brief DB
    • Link to R3 packet and Product ID
    • Tag angles used
  6. Update ClickUp Task:
    • Move from "Copy_Queue" ‚Üí "Copy_Review"
    • Add comment with Notion link
    • Tag Creative Director for review

Outputs

  • Notion Creative Brief with 4-6 hooks, 2-3 scripts, 3-5 CTAs, captions
  • ClickUp task in "Copy_Review" status
  • Discord notification in #p2-briefs

Quality Checks

  • All hooks are under 10 words (scroll-stop friendly)
  • Scripts follow Hook ‚Üí Agitate ‚Üí Solution ‚Üí CTA structure
  • At least 2 different angle approaches (diversity)
  • Language matches target demo (no jargon for mass market)
  • CTAs are action-oriented and urgent
  • All fields in Notion Creative Brief are populated

Pro Tip: First 3 Seconds Rule

60-80% of users scroll past in 3 seconds. Your hook must create a "pattern interrupt" immediately. Test: Can you say the hook out loud in under 3 seconds? If not, cut words.

Workflow 2: P2 Copy Iteration (Performance-Driven)

Trigger

Product passes P1 (P1_PASS status) OR Creative Director requests P2 variants

Inputs

  • P1 performance data (CTR, CPC, CPA by creative variant)
  • Original P1 Creative Brief
  • Creative Director notes on what worked/didn't work
  • Media Buyer feedback (if available)

Actions

  1. Analyze P1 Data (10 min):
    • Identify which hooks/angles had highest CTR
    • Note which scripts led to depth events (ATC/IC)
    • Look for qualitative signals (comments, DMs if shared by P3)
  2. Generate P2 Variants (30 min):
    • Double down on winning angle with 2-3 new hooks
    • Test 1-2 "risky" hypotheses (counter-positioning, polarization)
    • Create 1-2 new script variants focused on conversion (more benefit detail, urgency)
  3. Update Brief & ClickUp:
    • Add P2 copy section to Notion Creative Brief
    • Move ClickUp task to "Copy_P2_Active" status
    • Tag for Creative Director approval

Outputs

  • 2-4 new P2 copy variants in Notion
  • ClickUp task updated to P2 phase

Quality Checks

  • P2 variants are meaningfully different from P1 (not just word swaps)
  • At least one variant tests a new angle or objection handling
  • Scripts are conversion-focused (more benefit detail, stronger CTAs)
Workflow 3: Copy QA & Approval Loop

Trigger

You move ClickUp task to "Copy_Review" OR Creative Director requests revisions

Actions

  1. Self-QA (5 min):
    • Read all copy out loud (catch awkward phrasing)
    • Check for typos, grammar issues
    • Verify all links/product names are correct
  2. Tag Creative Director:
    • Add ClickUp comment: "@CreativeDirector Copy ready for review: [Notion link]"
    • Post in #p2-briefs: "üîç Copy Review: [Product ID] - [Brief Link]"
  3. Handle Feedback (within 2 hours of receiving):
    • If approved ‚Üí Move to "Copy_Approved" and trigger editor handoff
    • If revisions ‚Üí Make changes, re-QA, re-submit
    • If rejected ‚Üí Escalate to Creative Director for new angle direction

SLA

From "Copy_Review" to "Copy_Approved" should take <24 hours (including Creative Director review time). If delayed, escalate to PM.

Workflow 4: Angle Library Maintenance

Trigger

Weekly (Friday afternoon) OR when P1 performance data shows clear winners

Purpose

Build a living database of proven hooks, angles, and copy patterns to accelerate future briefs.

Actions

  1. Review P1 results from the week
  2. Identify hooks/scripts with CTR >1.5% or CPC <$1.00
  3. Add to Notion Angle Library with:
    • Product niche
    • Angle category (problem-agitate, curiosity, authority, etc.)
    • Actual copy text
    • Performance metrics
  4. Tag patterns (e.g., "temporal urgency", "before/after", "secret reveal")

Why This Matters

The Angle Library turns your work into compounding leverage. Instead of starting from zero on every brief, you reference proven patterns and adapt them. This is how you go from 10 briefs/week to 20 without burning out.

AI Usage & Prompt Patterns

This role leverages AI (ChatGPT, Claude) heavily for rapid iteration and volume. Below are ready-to-use prompts and best practices.

Core Principle: AI Accelerates, You Curate

AI generates 10-15 raw hooks in 30 seconds. You refine to the best 4-6 based on strategy, performance data, and brand fit. Never ship AI output raw without manual refinement.

Prompt 1: P1 Hook Generation 
You are a direct response copywriter specializing in high-CTR hooks for Meta/TikTok ads.

Product: [Product Name]
Niche: [E.g., Pet Health, Sleep, Kitchen]
AOV: $[XX]
Primary Problem: [From R3 packet]
Target Demo: [E.g., Dog owners 35-55, US]

Generate 12 scroll-stopping ad hooks that:
1. Are under 10 words each
2. Use specific numbers or dramatic claims
3. Address the primary problem directly
4. Create curiosity or pattern interrupt
5. Avoid generic phrases like "game changer" or "revolutionary"

Format as a numbered list. Prioritize hooks that would work in first 3 seconds of video.

Human Checkpoint

  • Select 4-6 hooks that align with R3 angle strategy
  • Remove any that feel too hype or unverifiable
  • Ensure diversity (2 problem-agitate, 2 curiosity, 1-2 authority)
Prompt 2: Script Template Generation 
You are writing a 30-second direct response ad script for Meta/TikTok.

Product: [Product Name]
Hook (already chosen): "[Hook text]"
Primary Benefit: [From R3 packet]
Key Features: [List 2-3]
CTA: [Shop Now / Get Yours / Limited Stock]

Write a 30-second script following this structure:
- Hook (3 seconds)
- Problem Agitate (7 seconds)
- Solution Introduction (8 seconds)
- Benefit Stack (8 seconds)
- CTA + Urgency (4 seconds)

Use simple language, short sentences, and benefit-focused framing. Include [VO] and [Scene] notes for the editor.

Human Checkpoint

  • Verify timing adds up to ~30 seconds when read aloud
  • Remove any jargon or complex phrases
  • Ensure Scene notes are clear for editors
Prompt 3: CTA Variant Generation 
Generate 5 CTA variations for a [Product Type] ad targeting [Demo].

Current CTA: "Shop Now"

Requirements:
- Action-oriented verbs
- Some urgency without being aggressive
- Mix of direct (Shop Now) and softer (Learn More) approaches
- Under 4 words each

Format as numbered list.
Prompt 4: Caption Generation (TikTok/IG) 
Write 3 social media caption variants for this product ad:

Product: [Product Name]
Hook: "[Hook text]"
Platform: TikTok/Instagram
Tone: Casual, benefit-focused, minimal emoji

Requirements:
- 1-2 sentences max
- Include 1-2 relevant hashtags
- Drive to link in bio / shop now
- No clickbait or fake urgency

Format as numbered list.
Prompt 5: P2 Iteration (Performance-Driven) 
Based on P1 performance data, generate 4 new hook variants that iterate on the winning angle.

Winning Hook: "[Best performing hook]"
CTR: [X.X%]
Why it worked: [Pattern analysis - e.g., specific number, dramatic claim, problem-focused]

Losing Hook: "[Lowest CTR hook]"
Why it failed: [Pattern analysis]

Generate 4 new hooks that:
- Double down on what worked in the winner
- Avoid patterns from the loser
- Test small variations (different numbers, different framing)
- Stay under 10 words

AI Usage Rules

  • Never skip human review: All AI output must be manually refined before handoff
  • No sensitive data in prompts: Don't paste full R3 packets with supplier info or pricing details
  • Track what works: When AI generates a hook that passes P1, save the prompt pattern to Angle Library
  • Iterate prompts: If AI output is consistently off-target, refine your prompts, don't just regenerate
