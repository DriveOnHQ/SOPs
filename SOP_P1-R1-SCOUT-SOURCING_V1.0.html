üéØRole Purpose & Context

Why this role exists: The CaniCol model tests 45-60 products/month at tight budgets ($40-60 P1, $70-120 P1+P2). This only works if we have a fast, high-volume R1 pipeline that filters out unviable products BEFORE expensive research and creative work begins. You are the first quality gate after R0 idea dumping.

Your job is breadth with intelligent filtering. You turn raw product ideas (from R0 scouts, trend feeds, spy tools) into vetted R1 packets that have:

  • Confirmed supplier availability with realistic pricing
  • Clear market plausibility (not just "trending" but sellable)
  • Basic logistics sanity (MOQ, lead time, shipping windows)
  • Clean data recorded in Product OS for R2 researchers

You are NOT responsible for:

  • Deep competitive research (that's R2)
  • Final R3 go/no-go decisions (that's PI Lead Strategist)
  • Creative angle development (that's P2)
Canon Anchor: This role supports the CaniCol v4.0 lean-volume model (L1-01). By maintaining a clean R1 funnel with supplier verification, you enable downstream researchers to focus on depth rather than basic sourcing grunt work.

Economic Constraints (from L1-01, L2-05)

  • P1 budget: $40-$60 per product
  • P1+P2 total: $70-$120 (standard $80-$100)
  • This means products must have realistic AOV and margin potential to work at these budgets

  • R1 WIP cap: 8-10 packets in progress
  • Supplier checks: ~6 active at a time
  • Weekly throughput: 20-40 R1 packets completed

Tool Stack (from L2/L3)

  • Approved tools only: TikTok, AliExpress/Alibaba, CJ, Notion, ClickUp, Google Drive
  • No unapproved tools: Airtable, Asana, random product research SaaS without PM approval
Common Mistake: Spending too much time on deep research at R1. Your job is fast filtering and supplier verification. If you find yourself spending 2+ hours on one product at R1, you're doing R2 work‚Äîhand it to R2 researchers instead.

‚öôÔ∏èCore Workflows

These are your 3 main workflows. Each has specific inputs, steps, and quality gates.

Trigger

New R0 idea appears in ClickUp "R0 Ideas" list OR Discord #p1-r0-idea-dump

Inputs

  • Product concept (name, category, link to inspiration)
  • Source signal (TikTok video, ad library screenshot, spy tool report)
  • Rough price point estimate (if available)

Steps

  1. Quick market check (10 min):
    • Is this product type selling on Amazon/similar platforms?
    • Are there existing reviews/social proof?
    • Does it fit a clear pain point or desire?
  2. Initial supplier search (15 min):
    • Search AliExpress, Alibaba for similar products
    • Find 2-3 potential suppliers
    • Screenshot price ranges and product images
  3. Plausibility assessment (5 min):
    • Can we realistically sell this at 2-3x supplier cost?
    • Are there obvious compliance/shipping red flags?
    • Does MOQ look reasonable (typically <500 units)?
  4. Decision:
    • KILL: Move to ClickUp status "R1_FAIL", add kill reason in comments
    • PROMOTE: Create Product ID, move to "R1 Shortlist", proceed to Workflow 2

Outputs

  • ClickUp task moved to R1 Shortlist with Product_ID assigned
  • Initial supplier screenshots saved to Drive /02_P1_Product_Intel/Products/[ProductID]/01_Research_Raw/
  • Basic plausibility notes in ClickUp task description

Quality Check

Before promoting to R1, ask: "If I handed this to an R2 researcher right now, could they immediately start deep research without re-doing supplier search?"

Kill Criteria: No viable suppliers found, obvious compliance issues, market too saturated with no angle, pricing math doesn't work even optimistically.

Trigger

Product promoted to R1 Shortlist and needs formal supplier validation

Inputs

  • Product_ID from ClickUp
  • 2-3 potential suppliers identified in Workflow 1
  • Product specification requirements (if any from R0 notes)

Steps

  1. Contact top 2 suppliers (30 min):
    • Send inquiry via AliExpress message or Alibaba
    • Request: MOQ, unit price at different quantities, lead time, shipping options, payment terms
    • Ask about customization options (packaging, branding) if relevant
  2. Document supplier responses (15 min):
    • Create entry in Notion Products OS ‚Üí Supplier Info section
    • Required fields: Supplier Name, Contact, MOQ, Unit Price, Lead Time, Shipping Method, Notes
    • Upload supplier quotes/screenshots to Drive /02_P1_Product_Intel/Products/[ProductID]/03_Supplier_Docs/
  3. Calculate basic unit economics (10 min):
    • Supplier cost + shipping estimate = landed cost
    • Potential retail price (2.5-3x landed cost for dropship/test products)
    • Does this give room for P1 test profitability?
  4. Flag issues (5 min):
    • If MOQ > 500 units ‚Üí flag as "High MOQ Risk"
    • If lead time > 4 weeks ‚Üí flag as "Slow Sourcing"
    • If no responsive suppliers ‚Üí escalate or kill
  5. Update ClickUp task:
    • Set custom field "Supplier_Validated" = Yes
    • Add supplier summary in task comments
    • Move status to R1_COMPLETE

Outputs

  • Complete supplier data in Notion Products OS
  • Supplier documents in Drive folder
  • ClickUp task ready for R2 handoff

Quality Check

R2 researcher should be able to see: exact supplier pricing, realistic lead time, and any sourcing risks WITHOUT having to contact suppliers again.

Trigger

R1 packet complete (supplier validated, data recorded)

Inputs

  • Completed R1 ClickUp task with all data
  • Notion Products OS entry populated
  • Drive folder with supplier docs

Steps

  1. Final quality sweep (5 min):
    • Verify Product_ID is correct in all systems
    • Check that all required Notion fields are filled
    • Ensure Drive folder structure matches template
  2. ClickUp handoff:
    • Move task from "R1 Shortlist" list to "R2 Dossiers" list
    • Change status to R2_PENDING
    • Assign to next available R2 researcher (or leave unassigned for pull system)
  3. Discord notification:
    • Post in #p1-r1-shortlist channel:
    ‚úÖ R1 Complete: [Product_ID] - [Product Name]
Supplier: [Best Supplier Name]
MOQ: [X units] | Cost: $[Y] | Lead: [Z days]
Notion: [Link]
ClickUp: [Link]
Ready for R2 deep research

Outputs

  • ClickUp task in R2 queue
  • Discord handoff notification sent
  • Clean data trail for R2 researcher to pick up

ü§ñAI Usage & Prompt Patterns

AI can speed up market research and supplier communication. Use it to augment your judgment, not replace it.

When to Use AI

  • Product category research: "What are common pain points in [category]?"
  • Competitive analysis: "Summarize Amazon reviews for [similar product]"
  • Supplier communication drafts: "Write a professional inquiry to supplier about MOQ and lead time"
  • Pricing sanity checks: "Is $X retail realistic for [product type]?"

Ready-to-Copy Prompts

I'm evaluating a product for dropshipping viability: [Product Name/Description].

Context:
- Target market: US consumers
- Budget constraints: Must be profitable at $40-60 P1 test spend
- Typical margin: 2.5-3x landed cost for test products

Please analyze:
1. Is there clear demand/pain point for this product?
2. What's the typical price range on Amazon/major retailers?
3. Are there obvious compliance or shipping red flags?
4. Quick gut check: viable or not worth testing?

Keep your response to 3-4 bullet points. I need speed over depth.
I need to contact a supplier on AliExpress/Alibaba for [Product Name].

Write a professional inquiry message asking for:
1. Minimum Order Quantity (MOQ)
2. Unit price at different quantities (100, 200, 500 units)
3. Production lead time
4. Shipping options to USA and estimated costs
5. Payment terms and methods
6. Possibility of custom packaging/branding

Keep it concise and professional. The supplier should be able to respond quickly with specific numbers.
Product: [Name/Description]
Supplier Cost: $[X]
Potential Retail: $[Y]

Analyze:
1. What do similar products sell for on Amazon/direct-to-consumer sites?
2. Is my proposed retail price ($[Y]) in a reasonable range?
3. What's the typical margin in this category?
4. Red flags or considerations for pricing strategy?

Focus on US market, DTC/dropshipping context. Keep it practical.

Human Checkpoints

Never blindly trust AI for:

  • Final supplier selection (always verify quotes yourself)
  • Compliance/legal assessments (escalate to PM or legal if unsure)
  • Go/no-go decisions (use AI for research, but you make the call)
Pro Tip: Use AI for the first draft of supplier messages, but always personalize before sending. Suppliers can tell when you're using generic templates.

üö®Escalation & Decision Rights

Know what you own, what needs approval, and when to escalate.

Decisions You Can Make Alone

  • ‚úÖ Kill a product at R1 if suppliers are non-viable (document reason in ClickUp)
  • ‚úÖ Choose between multiple suppliers (pick best based on price/MOQ/lead time)
  • ‚úÖ Re-prioritize your daily task order based on pipeline needs
  • ‚úÖ Decide how deep to research at R1 (stay fast, don't over-research)

Decisions Requiring PI Lead or PM

  • üî∫ Promoting a product to R2 that has unusual characteristics (very high MOQ, exotic sourcing, complex logistics)
  • üî∫ Requesting access to new supplier platforms or paid tools
