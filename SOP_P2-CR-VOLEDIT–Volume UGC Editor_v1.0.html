2. Role Purpose & Context
Why this role exists and how it fits into CaniCol v4.0

Strategic Purpose

The Volume / UGC Editor is the execution engine of the Creative Factory (Pod 2). This role exists to generate high-volume, UGC-style creative assets that support DriveOn Commerce's lean-testing philosophy under the CaniCol v4.0 model.

With ~45‚Äì60 products entering P1/P2 testing each month and tight budget constraints ($40‚Äì$60 per product for P1, $70‚Äì$120 total for P1+P2), the Volume Editor must produce fast, cost-effective, scroll-stopping video ads that look authentic and native to social feeds.

Economic Context

This role directly impacts the economic viability of the testing pipeline:

  • Test Bank Discipline: With a monthly test bank of $4,000‚Äì$5,500 for P1+P2 combined, creative costs must stay lean. High-volume editing enables multiple creative variants per product without breaking budget.
  • Hit Rate Support: Only 4‚Äì6% of products (~2‚Äì4/month) become true winners. Fast creative iteration increases the odds of finding winning angles early.
  • Portfolio Thinking: Most products fail. The Volume Editor's speed and efficiency ensure that failures are learned from quickly and cheaply, while winners get rapid creative reinforcement.

Pod 2 (Creative Factory) Context

The Volume Editor sits within Pod 2, which transforms product research packets into testable creative assets. The pod operates as a factory:

  • Upstream: Receives product packets and creative briefs from P1 (Product Intelligence) and P2-CR-STRAT (Creative Strategist).
  • Internal: Works alongside Creative Strategists, Script Writers, and Creative QA.
  • Downstream: Delivers finished assets to P3 (Media & Store) for campaign setup and testing.

Why "Volume" and Why "UGC"

Volume: This role is optimized for throughput, not artistry. You are not creating Super Bowl ads. You are creating testable hypotheses in video form at scale.

UGC (User-Generated Content) Style: UGC-style ads perform disproportionately well in dropshipping because they feel authentic, not salesy. They blend into social feeds, reduce ad fatigue, and lower CPMs. This role masters the UGC aesthetic: raw, relatable, mobile-first, scroll-stopping.

Core Philosophy: Speed and volume beat perfection. Your job is to produce good-enough-to-test assets quickly, learn from data, and iterate. Perfectionism is the enemy of throughput in this role.

Workflow 1: Daily Asset Queue Processing

Trigger

Start of each workday (9:00 AM) or when new high-priority brief arrives in #p2-creative-queue.

Inputs

  • ClickUp "Editing Queue" list (tasks assigned to you)
  • Discord #p2-creative-queue (new briefs and urgent requests)
  • Notion "Product Packets" DB (product context)
  • Google Drive /Creative_Briefs/ folder (detailed creative specs)

Steps

  1. Open ClickUp "Editing Queue" list. Filter by Assignee = You, Status = "Queued".
  2. Sort tasks by Priority (High ‚Üí Low) and Due Date (Soonest first).
  3. For each task, check linked Product_ID in Notion "Product Packets" to understand product context.
  4. Scan Discord #p2-creative-queue for overnight messages. Flag any urgent requests (e.g., "P1 launching in 12 hours, need 3 variants ASAP").
  5. Build today's priority list: (1) Urgent/deadline-driven tasks first, (2) High-priority P1 launches, (3) P2 creative scale-ups, (4) Standard queue items.
  6. Update ClickUp: Move top 6‚Äì10 tasks from "Queued" to "In Progress". Add comment: "Starting today [Date], ETA delivery by EOD."
  7. Download raw assets from Google Drive for priority tasks: go to /Raw_Assets/[Product_ID]/, download video clips, images, scripts.
  8. Stage raw assets locally in organized folders: /Local_Workspace/[Product_ID]/.
  9. Post brief standup in #p2-creative-queue: "Morning queue loaded. Priority today: [Product IDs]. ETA: [X] assets by EOD."

Outputs

  • Prioritized task list (6‚Äì10 tasks for the day)
  • ClickUp statuses updated ("In Progress")
  • Raw assets staged locally and ready to edit
  • Standup message posted in Discord

Quality Checks

  • ‚úì No P1 launch-critical tasks missed in priority ranking
  • ‚úì All raw asset files downloaded and accessible
  • ‚úì ClickUp comments added to confirm task ownership

Handoff

None ‚Äì Internal workflow. This sets up your editing pipeline for the day.

Workflow 2: UGC-Style Video Editing (Brief to Delivery)

Trigger

Creative Strategist delivers brief + raw assets. Task appears in ClickUp "Editing Queue" with status "Queued".

Inputs

  • Creative Brief (Google Doc or ClickUp description): hook, CTA, product angles, target duration, style notes
  • Raw assets in Google Drive: /Raw_Assets/[Product_ID]/ (video clips, B-roll, product images, voiceover scripts)
  • Product context from Notion "Product Packets" DB (AOV, niche, unique value prop)
  • Brand guidelines from L2-08 Master Brand Platform (tone, visual style, forbidden patterns)

Steps

  1. Read creative brief fully. Note key requirements: hook style (problem-agitate, curiosity gap, direct CTA), video length (15‚Äì30 sec), music mood, on-screen text requirements.
  2. Open editing software (CapCut for fast edits, Premiere Pro for complex projects). Create new project: [Product_ID]_Variant_[A/B/C].
  3. Import raw assets into project. Organize timeline: Intro (hook, 2‚Äì4 sec) ‚Üí Body (product demo, 8‚Äì15 sec) ‚Üí Close (CTA, 3‚Äì5 sec).
  4. Edit for UGC aesthetic: Use jump cuts, slight camera shake (if authentic), casual pacing. Avoid over-polishing. Think "real person sharing a find," not "brand commercial."
  5. Add on-screen text overlays for key points: Use bold, high-contrast fonts (white text with black stroke or vice versa). Keep text concise (3‚Äì5 words per frame).
  6. Sync background music: Use royalty-free tracks from approved library. Music should enhance, not overpower. Fade in/out smoothly.
  7. Add CTA at end: "Link in bio" / "Tap to shop" / "Check comments for link" (avoid salesy language).
  8. Self-QA before export: Watch full edit 2‚Äì3 times. Check for: typos in text overlays, audio sync issues, abrupt cuts, file name accuracy.
  9. Export settings: Format: MP4 (H.264), Resolution: 1080x1920 (9:16 vertical), Frame rate: 30fps, Bitrate: Medium-High (~5‚Äì8 Mbps), File size target: <30MB.
  10. Save exported file locally with naming convention: CRT-[Test_ID]-[Variant_ID]-[HookType].mp4. Example: CRT-TST-PRD-20251208-042-P101-A-ProblemAgitate.mp4.

Outputs

  • 1 finished video asset per variant (typically 2‚Äì4 variants per product)
  • File saved locally with correct naming convention
  • Project file saved for future revisions

Quality Checks

  • ‚úì Video matches brief specifications (hook, CTA, duration)
  • ‚úì UGC aesthetic achieved (authentic, mobile-native, not overly polished)
  • ‚úì No typos in on-screen text
  • ‚úì Audio levels balanced (music not overpowering voiceover/dialogue)
  • ‚úì Export specs correct (9:16, <30MB, 30fps)
  • ‚úì File naming convention followed exactly

Handoff

FROM: Volume Editor (you)
TO: Self (upload & organize phase, see Workflow 3) ‚Üí then to P2-CR-QA (Creative QA)
Tool: Local filesystem ‚Üí Google Drive ‚Üí ClickUp status update

Workflow 4: Revision Processing & Re-Delivery

Trigger

QA flags asset(s) with issues. ClickUp task moves to "Revision Needed" status. Discord notification appears in #p2-revisions.

Inputs

  • QA feedback: ClickUp comment or Discord message detailing issues (typo, audio sync, brand violation, etc.)
  • Original project file (saved during initial edit)
  • Original exported asset (for reference)

Steps

  1. Check Discord #p2-revisions and ClickUp "Revision Needed" list. Identify flagged assets.
  2. Read QA feedback carefully. Categorize issues: (1) Critical (brand violation, major error), (2) Standard (typo, minor audio issue), (3) Minor polish (optional improvement suggestion).
  3. Prioritize critical issues first. If multiple assets need revision, batch similar fixes (e.g., fix all typos in one session, all audio sync issues in another).
  4. Open original project file in editing software. Make required changes. Do NOT over-revise; stick to QA's specific notes.
  5. Re-export with same settings as original. Save with same file name (overwrite old file in local workspace).
  6. Upload revised file to Google Drive, overwriting previous version. Update "Date Modified" in Notion "Creative Assets" DB.
  7. Update ClickUp: Move task from "Revision Needed" to "QA Review" (re-submission). Add comment: "Revision complete. Changes: [list fixes]. Re-uploaded to Drive."
  8. Post update in Discord #p2-revisions: "Revisions complete for [Product IDs]. Re-submitted to QA."

Outputs

  • Revised video asset (same file name, updated content)
  • Updated file in Google Drive
  • Notion DB updated (Date Modified)
  • ClickUp task re-submitted to QA Review
  • Discord update posted

Quality Checks

  • ‚úì All QA-requested changes implemented
  • ‚úì No new issues introduced during revision
  • ‚úì File name unchanged (to maintain linking)
  • ‚úì Drive file updated (not duplicated)

Handoff

FROM: P2-CR-VOLEDIT-01 (Volume Editor, you)
TO: P2-CR-QA (Creative QA, re-review)
Tool: ClickUp, Google Drive, Discord
Location: Same as Workflow 3 handoff

Workflow 5: Batch Multi-Variant Test Setup

Trigger

Creative Strategist requests multiple variants for A/B testing (common for P2 scale-ups or high-priority P1 launches).

Inputs

  • Creative brief specifying variant count (typically 3‚Äì5 variants) and variant types (different hooks, CTAs, or visual styles)
  • Single set of raw assets (video clips, images, voiceover)
  • Variant matrix from Creative Strategist: Hook A / Hook B / Hook C √ó CTA 1 / CTA 2

Steps

  1. Review variant matrix. Understand differences between each variant (e.g., Variant A = Problem-Agitate hook, Variant B = Curiosity Gap hook, Variant C = Direct CTA hook).
  2. Create master project file with base edit (shared body section). Duplicate project file for each variant.
  3. For each variant, edit only the differing sections: swap hook intro, change CTA ending, or adjust on-screen text to match variant theme.
  4. Keep body/demo section identical across variants (unless brief specifies otherwise). This ensures clean A/B test data.
  5. Export all variants in batch. Use naming convention: CRT-[Test_ID]-[Variant_Letter]-[HookType].mp4. Example:
    • CRT-TST-001-P101-A-ProblemAgitate.mp4
    • CRT-TST-001-P101-B-CuriosityGap.mp4
    • CRT-TST-001-P101-C-DirectCTA.mp4
  6. Self-QA all variants: Watch each variant once, confirm only intended sections differ. Check for typos in variant-specific text.
  7. Upload all variants to Google Drive: /Creative_Assets/[Product_ID]/Variants/
  8. Log all variants in Notion "Creative Assets" DB (one entry per variant). Include Variant_Letter and Variant_Type fields.
  9. Update ClickUp: Move task to "QA Review". Add comment: "Multi-variant batch complete. [X] variants uploaded. Drive: [link]."
  10. Post handoff in Discord #p2-asset-delivery: "Multi-variant batch ready ‚Äì Product: [ID], Variants: [A, B, C], Total: [X] assets."

Outputs

  • 3‚Äì5 video variants (per brief specs)
  • All variants uploaded to Drive
  • All variants logged in Notion
  • ClickUp task moved to QA Review
  • Discord handoff notification posted

Quality Checks

  • ‚úì All variants exported and uploaded (no missing variants)
  • ‚úì Variant differences are clear and intentional (not accidental)
  • ‚úì Shared sections (body) are identical across variants
  • ‚úì File naming reflects variant letter and type
  • ‚úì Notion entries distinguish variants clearly

Handoff

FROM: P2-CR-VOLEDIT-01 (Volume Editor, you)
TO: P2-CR-QA ‚Üí then to P3-MB (Media Buyer for testing setup)
Tool: ClickUp, Notion, Google Drive, Discord

8. AI Usage & Prompt Patterns
How to leverage AI tools while maintaining human quality control

Philosophy: AI as Copilot, Not Autopilot

AI tools can accelerate certain editing tasks, but you remain responsible for final quality, brand compliance, and creative judgment. AI outputs must always pass through human review.

Approved AI Use Cases

You may use AI for the following tasks within this role:

10. Escalation & Decision Rights
What you can decide alone, and when to escalate

Decisions You Can Make Independently

You have full authority to make these decisions without approval:

  • Editing techniques: Cut styles, pacing choices, transition effects (as long as UGC aesthetic is maintained)
  • On-screen text placement: Where and how to position text overlays (as long as legible and brand-compliant)
  • Music selection: Choose tracks from approved library based on brief mood requirements
  • Creative interpretation: How to execute brief details (e.g., if brief says "energetic hook," you decide the specific execution)
  • Variant sequencing: Order in which you tackle editing queue (within priority constraints)
  • Self-QA standards: Your personal quality checklist can be stricter than the baseline (but not looser)
  • Minor revisions: Typo fixes, small audio adjustments, re-exports due to technical issues

Decisions Requiring Pod 2 Lead Approval

You must escalate and get approval for:

  • Deviating from brief: If you believe the brief's creative direction won't work, escalate before making major changes
  • Brand guideline edge cases: If unsure whether something violates L2-08 Master Brand Platform, ask before proceeding
  • Missed deadlines: If you cannot meet a P1 launch deadline, escalate immediately (as soon as you know, not when it's too late)
  • WIP cap overrun: If queue depth forces you over WIP limits, escalate to discuss prioritization or resource allocation
  • Tool/workflow changes: If you want to adopt a new tool or significantly change workflow, propose via Pod 2 Lead (not unilaterally)
  • Quality vs speed tradeoffs: If hitting throughput targets requires compromising quality, escalate to discuss acceptable tradeoffs

Decisions Requiring Founder / Canon Owner Approval

The following are never decided at your level or even Pod Lead level‚Äîthese require L0/L1 canon changes:

  • Changing testing budgets or gate definitions
  • Altering WIP caps or throughput targets
  • Introducing new core tools (e.g., replacing ClickUp with Asana)
  • Modifying brand positioning or creative guardrails

If you believe such changes are needed, you can propose them, but they go through the Canon Change Request (CCR) process, not operational decision flow.
