1. Purpose & Context

Why This Role Exists

The CaniCol model (L1-01) produces 2‚Äì4 true winners per month from ~45‚Äì60 tests. Most products fail. The few that reach P3_WINNER or TIER_A status deserve premium brand treatment‚Äînot generic 1-page test stores.

You are the architect who transforms validated winners into:

  • Durable brand experiences with coherent narratives
  • High-conversion stores optimized for AOV and LTV
  • Scalable systems that don't require founder heroics
  • Premium positioning aligned with DriveOn's anti-hype thesis

How This Supports CaniCol Economics

Winners fund the testing engine. By maximizing AOV, LTV, and conversion rate, you:

  • Expand margins to protect the $4,000‚Äì$5,500/month test bank
  • Create brand equity that compounds over time
  • Reduce reliance on paid acquisition through organic lift
  • Build portfolio assets that can support 2‚Äì4 winners simultaneously
‚öñÔ∏èCanon Anchor

This role implements L0-02 (Product & Brand Architecture) and L2-06 (Scaling Tiers). You work exclusively on products that have passed P2 and entered P3_WINNER or TIER_A_WINNER status per L2-05 testing thresholds.

Brand Architecture Rules

From L0-02 & L0-02B: Every brand store must route to one of three lanes:

Lane C1 ‚Äî DriveOn Standard (Flagship Wellness)

Recovery-first performance wellness products that align with anti-noise, systems-first positioning. Products must support clear routines and honest, non-hype claims.

Lane C2 ‚Äî 1P Micro-Brands (Category-Focused)

Distinct category-specific brands (e.g., posture brand, recovery tools, golf accessories). Must have multi-year niche potential and earn their own identity.

Lane C3 ‚Äî General/SEO Catalog (Monetization)

Profitable but "boring" residual winners that don't fit C1/C2 narratives. Optimized for organic/SEO and long-tail sales with low operator attention.

‚öñÔ∏èBrand Routing Decision

Lane routing is NOT automatic. Before building, confirm routing with Founder/Integrator based on: economics (margin, LTV potential), brand fit (alignment with DriveOn thesis), category potential (multi-year roadmap), and operational load.

Creative & Brand Guardrails

From L2-08 (Master Brand Platform): All brand stores must express:

  • Clarity Over Noise: Reduce complexity into just-enough structure. Name tradeoffs instead of hiding them.
  • Runnable Systems, Not One-Off Tips: Everything resolves into executable steps for imperfect humans with jobs, families, fatigue.
  • Tested & Simulated, Not Just Theorized: Explicit about what is tested, what is modeled, what is hypothesis.

Forbidden:

  • Countdown timers, fake urgency, or scarcity
  • Dark patterns or deceptive UX
  • "Miracle" positioning or unproven claims
  • Generic wellness vibes without substance

Workflow 2 ‚Äî CRO Optimization (Existing Winner)

Trigger

Store has been live 14‚Äì30 days; baseline conversion data available; CVR or AOV below target.

Inputs

  • GA4 analytics: CVR, bounce rate, exit pages, user flow
  • Heatmaps/recordings (Hotjar/Microsoft Clarity)
  • Meta/TikTok ad performance: CTR, landing page view rate
  • Customer feedback or CS objections

Steps

1. Data Analysis (2‚Äì4 hours)
  • Identify highest-exit pages and drop-off points
  • Review heatmaps: What's getting clicked? What's ignored?
  • Analyze mobile vs desktop performance gaps
  • Check cart abandonment rate and reasons
  • Document findings in Notion CRO project page
2. Hypothesis Formation (1‚Äì2 hours)
  • Prioritize changes by expected impact √ó ease
  • Common wins: Clearer CTAs, trust elements, faster load, simplified checkout
  • Create test plan: Control vs Variant A (vs Variant B if multivariate)
3. Implementation (4‚Äì8 hours)
  • Build variant pages or A/B test in Shopify
  • Set up split test (Google Optimize / Shopify Scripts / custom)
  • QA both control and variant flows
  • Set success metrics: CVR, AOV, revenue per session
4. Monitor & Report (Ongoing)
  • Run test for 7‚Äì14 days or 100+ conversions per variant
  • Check statistical significance (p < 0.05)
  • Document results in Notion CRO page
  • Implement winner permanently or iterate on loser

Outputs

  • Implemented CRO changes (test or permanent)
  • Notion CRO page with hypothesis, results, learnings
  • Updated store if test wins (backup old version)
  • Discord summary in #p3-stores-brand

Quality Bar Standards

Brand Narrative (Good vs Bad)

‚úÖ GOOD:

"Sleep Guard Pro supports your recovery system with temperature-regulated sleep. No hype‚Äîjust deep sleep physics: lower core temp = faster sleep onset. Validated in 60-day test with 2,400 users."

Why it works: Clear problem (recovery), clear mechanism (temp regulation), tested claim with specifics.

‚ùå BAD:

"Revolutionary sleep technology! Scientists HATE this one trick! Transform your life overnight with our miracle cooling system! Limited time: 70% off!"

Why it fails: Hype language, unproven claims, fake urgency. Contradicts DriveOn anti-noise positioning.

Mobile UX (Good vs Bad)

‚úÖ GOOD:

  • Product images load in < 1s on 4G
  • Add to Cart button always visible (sticky on scroll)
  • Tap targets ‚â• 44px (iOS accessibility standard)
  • Checkout completes in 3 taps (no forced account creation)
  • FAQ accordion expands smoothly without jank

‚ùå BAD:

  • Hero image is 5MB, blocks rendering for 8+ seconds
  • CTA button hidden below fold, requires scrolling to find
  • Small text (10px) unreadable on mobile without zoom
  • Checkout requires account creation before seeing total
  • Image galleries cause horizontal scroll (broken layout)

CRO Setup (Good vs Bad)

‚úÖ GOOD:

  • Meta Pixel fires Purchase event with correct value + currency
  • Exit-intent popup offers 10% off (first-time only)
  • Cart abandonment email sends after 1 hour with discount
  • Upsell appears in cart: "Complete your system" bundle
  • Trust badges: "30-day guarantee" + "4.8‚òÖ from 1,200 reviews"

‚ùå BAD:

  • Pixel not installed; no conversion tracking data
  • Popup appears instantly (before user reads anything)
  • No email capture; losing 70% of visitors permanently
  • No upsells or bundles; every sale is single-product
  • Generic "buy now" CTA; no trust or urgency elements

9. Escalation & Decision Rights

Decisions You Own (No Approval Needed)

  • Theme selection (within approved premium themes)
  • Store structure and navigation hierarchy
  • Product page layout and copy (within brand guidelines)
  • CRO test design and A/B variants
  • Collection organization and filters
  • Email/SMS flow setup (standard abandonment, post-purchase)
  • Minor design tweaks (colors, spacing, fonts within theme)

Decisions Requiring Pod Lead / Integrator Approval

  • Custom theme development (outside approved themes)
  • Significant infrastructure changes (domain migration, platform switch)
  • Budget over $500 for tools/plugins (e.g., premium page builder)
  • Launching store with known UX issues (trade-off decision)
  • Major brand narrative changes (affects positioning)

Decisions Requiring Founder / Canon Owner

  • Brand routing decision (C1 vs C2 vs C3)
  • Creating new micro-brand (C2) outside existing lanes
  • Brand positioning that deviates from DriveOn thesis
  • Legal/compliance concerns (claims, guarantees, refund policies)

When to Escalate

Performance Issues (Immediate)

  • Store CVR < 1% after 30 days (significantly below target)
  • Bounce rate > 70% consistently
  • Load time > 5s on mobile (critical UX failure)
  • Pixel tracking completely broken (no Purchase events firing)

Action: Post in #p3-stores-brand with @Integrator, include GA4 screenshot

Brand/Positioning Conflicts (Same Day)

  • Product claims cannot be validated (no test data, supplier unverifiable)
  • Creative assets use hype language that contradicts DriveOn positioning
  • Founder requests positioning that conflicts with canon brand guidelines

Action: Create Notion doc with conflict summary, tag Founder/Integrator

Resource/WIP Constraints (Within 24h)

  • Active builds exceed WIP cap (3+ active, all complex)
  • Simultaneous urgent requests from Media Buyer + Founder
  • Build requires skills outside your scope (custom app development)

Action: Post in #p4-pm-daily-standup, propose prioritization

Expected Response Times

Escalation Type To Expected Response
Critical performance issue Integrator < 4 hours
Brand routing decision Founder < 24 hours (async OK)
WIP/priority conflict PM / Integrator < 8 hours
Budget approval (> $500) Founder < 48 hours
