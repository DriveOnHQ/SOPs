Good vs Bad: Canon Fit Screening ‚ñº

‚úÖ Good Example

‚ùå Bad Example

‚úÖ Good Example

‚ùå Bad Example

(5 days later, candidate emails asking for update, no response)

Why this is bad: Ghosting kills candidate experience. Even a "we're still reviewing" is better than silence.

How KPIs Are Measured & Reported

You own a weekly hiring dashboard (Google Sheets or Notion dashboard) with:

  • Open Roles: Count by pod and status (Sourcing / Screening / Interviewing / Offer Pending)
  • Pipeline Velocity: Candidates advanced per stage this week
  • Time-to-Fill: Average days from posting to offer acceptance (rolling 30-day window)
  • Offer Acceptance Rate: Offers accepted √∑ total offers sent (rolling 60-day window)
  • 30-Day Retention: New hires still active √∑ total hires (trailing 30 days)

Reporting cadence:

  • Weekly: Post dashboard link in #p4-hiring (Fridays EOD)
  • Monthly: Detailed report to Founder/P4-OP-INTEGRATOR with trends, bottlenecks, hiring forecast
üí° KPI Tip: Lead Indicators vs Lag Indicators

Lead indicators (pipeline velocity, response time) tell you if you're on track before the final outcome.

Lag indicators (time-to-fill, retention) tell you what already happened.

Track both, but act on lead indicators to prevent lag indicator problems.

10. Escalation & Decision Rights

Why this section matters: Knowing when to decide vs when to escalate protects both you and the business. Never guess on big decisions; always escalate when in doubt.

Decisions You Own (No Escalation Needed)

  • Which candidates advance from resume screen to phone screen
  • Which candidates advance from phone screen to skills test
  • Scheduling interviews and coordinating with pod leads
  • Sending standard candidate communications (status updates, rejections, interview invites)
  • Updating ATS, ClickUp, and Notion with candidate progress
  • Posting job ads on approved platforms (Upwork, OnlineJobs, LinkedIn)
  • Using AI to draft content (as long as you human-review before sending)

Decisions Requiring PM/Integrator Approval

  • Hiring for a role NOT in L2-03 canon
  • Modifying comp ranges beyond approved bands
  • Creating custom comp structures (e.g., rev-share, equity)
  • Posting on new platforms not in L3 (e.g., Fiverr, Freelancer.com)
  • Extending deadlines or timelines beyond typical bands (e.g., >30 day time-to-fill)
  • Making offers without pod lead technical interview

Decisions Requiring Founder Approval

  • Creating new roles or role hybrids
  • Comp bands outside L2-03 approved ranges
  • Hiring for leadership/senior roles (P4-OP-INTEGRATOR, pod leads, etc.)
  • Making exceptions to NDA/security policy
  • Changing core hiring criteria or canon-fit standards

When to Escalate (Even If Not Explicitly Listed)

Escalate immediately if:

  • A candidate is asking for terms you're not sure are allowed
  • You're stuck on a decision for >2 hours
  • A hiring manager is pushing you to break canon constraints
  • A candidate is threatening legal action or making discrimination claims
  • You're unsure if a decision will set a bad precedent
