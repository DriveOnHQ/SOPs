üéØ Role Purpose & Context

Why this role exists: Winning paid ads create immediate revenue, but organic social creates compounding brand equity. This role transforms validated winners into lasting organic audiences.

Strategic Context

In the CaniCol v4.0 model:

  • 45‚Äì60 products enter P1/P2 testing monthly
  • 4‚Äì6% become true winners (2‚Äì4 products/month)
  • Winners deserve sustained organic visibility to maximize lifetime value
  • Organic content de-risks paid media dependency and builds trust

How This Role Supports DriveOn Economics

  1. Brand Equity Compounding: Organic followers = free impressions for future winners
  2. CPA Reduction: Organic traffic supplements paid, improving blended CAC
  3. Creative Asset Efficiency: Repurpose winning ads ‚Üí 3x content leverage
  4. Trust Signal: Active organic presence = credibility in paid funnel
  5. Winner Lane Support: Organic feeds brand stores (Lane C1/C2) with engaged audiences
üí° 2025 Best Practice

Modern audiences demand consistent organic presence. According to 2025 data, brands with regular posting schedules see 33% higher engagement rates. This role ensures DriveOn winners don't just test well‚Äîthey build audiences.

‚öñÔ∏è Canon Constraints

These constraints are non-negotiable and derived from L0‚ÄìL3 canon. All workflows must respect these boundaries.
‚öñÔ∏è Brand Platform Guardrails (L2-08)
  • Voice: Direct, calm, competent, grounded, systems-minded
  • Banned language: "Guaranteed results", "effortless", "secret hack", hype/FOMO language
  • Claims: All health/performance claims must be adequately substantiated
  • Visual style: Clean, modern, high contrast, restrained palette
  • No fake scarcity: Countdown timers, fake stock counters, invented urgency
‚öñÔ∏è WIP & Throughput Limits (L2-03)
  • Active brands: 3‚Äì5 maximum per 1.0 FTE
  • Content calendars: 3‚Äì4 active calendars
  • Posting cadence: 3‚Äì7 posts/week per channel (per brand)
  • Quality > quantity: Consistent, on-brand content beats sporadic volume
‚öñÔ∏è Activation Trigger

This role activates only after P2_PASS winners exist. Do not build organic channels for P1-only products. Wait for validated winners with proven economics and creative angles.

Content Repurposing Rules

  • Organic content should primarily repurpose winning paid ads
  • Avoid creating net-new content from scratch unless strategically justified
  • Use proven hooks, angles, and visuals from P2/P3 creatives
  • Adapt format (square ‚Üí 9:16, remove CTA overlays) but keep core message
‚ö†Ô∏è Common Failure Modes
  • Building organic presence for products that never pass P2
  • Posting inconsistently or abandoning channels mid-growth
  • Creating off-brand content that contradicts DriveOn positioning
  • Ignoring engagement (posting without community management)
  • Using trending audio/formats that don't align with brand voice

üí° 2025 Best Practice: Batching & Consistency

Workflow 2: Monthly Content Calendar Build

Trigger

Beginning of month OR new brand channel launch

Inputs

  • Last month's performance data (Notion analytics)
  • Available winning creatives from Drive
  • P3 campaign calendar (upcoming launches, promotions)
  • Brand seasonal hooks (holidays, industry events)

Steps

  1. Set monthly targets per brand:
    • Posts per week: 3‚Äì7 per channel (IG/TikTok/YT)
    • Content mix: 60% repurposed ads, 20% educational, 20% engagement/UGC
  2. Audit available assets:
    • Review winning P2/P3 ads in Drive
    • Identify top 10 best-performing creatives for repurposing
    • Note any asset gaps (need new graphics, B-roll, etc.)
  3. Map content pillars to calendar slots:
    • Monday: Educational (how-to, tips)
    • Wednesday: Product feature/benefit
    • Friday: Social proof (testimonial, UGC)
    • Adjust based on platform (TikTok = trends, IG = aesthetics, YT Shorts = snackable)
  4. Schedule specific posts in Notion:
    • Date, platform, content type, asset source
    • Draft caption (or prompt for AI-generated caption)
    • Status: Planned ‚Üí In Progress ‚Üí Scheduled ‚Üí Published
  5. Leave 20% calendar space open for:
    • Trending audio/challenges
    • Real-time campaign tie-ins
    • Community-generated content ideas
  6. Review with Creative Director (if first-time brand):
    • Share Notion calendar link
    • Confirm content mix aligns with brand strategy
    • Get approval on repurposing approach

Outputs

  • 30-day content calendar in Notion (3‚Äì5 brands)
  • Asset requests sent to Creative Director (if gaps exist)
  • ClickUp tasks created for each week's batch

Quality Checks

  • All brands have 2+ weeks scheduled ahead
  • Content mix follows 60/20/20 repurpose/education/engagement split
  • No two consecutive posts use the same creative
  • Captions follow L2-08 voice/tone (no banned language)
  • All posts have clear value proposition (not just product shots)
Workflow 3: Repurpose Winning Ad ‚Üí Organic Post

Trigger

Scheduled calendar slot needs content creation

Inputs

  • Winning ad file: /03_P2_Creative/Products/[ProductID]/04_Exports/P2_Tests/CRT-XXX.mp4
  • Ad performance data (CTR, engagement from P3 Media Buyer)
  • Brand voice guidelines (Notion or L2-08)

Steps

  1. Download winning ad from Drive
  2. Adapt for organic format:
    • Video: Remove CTA overlays, add subtitles if needed, adjust aspect ratio (9:16 for Reels/TikTok, 1:1 for IG feed)
    • Image: Remove promotional text, enhance for feed aesthetics
    • Carousel: Reformat slides, add value-first narrative
  3. Edit in CapCut or Canva:
    • Quick edits only (5‚Äì15 min per piece)
    • Add trending music (if brand-appropriate)
    • Use templates from Canva for consistency
  4. Write organic caption:
    • Hook (first line): value or curiosity
    • Body: explain benefit, tell story, or educate
    • CTA: soft (visit link in bio, follow for more, save for later)
    • No banned language from L2-08
    • Use AI prompt if needed (see AI Usage section)
  5. Add hashtags (5‚Äì10 per post):
    • Mix: 3 niche, 3 medium, 2 broad
    • Avoid spammy or off-brand tags
  6. Export and save:
    • Save to Drive: /03_P2_Creative/Products/[ProductID]/05_Organic_Content/
    • File naming: ORG-[ProductID]-[Platform]-[Date]-[Number].mp4
    • Example: ORG-PRD-20251208-042-IG-20251215-01.mp4
  7. Update Notion calendar:
    • Mark status: Planned ‚Üí In Progress ‚Üí Ready to Schedule
    • Attach Drive link to calendar entry

Outputs

  • 1 organic post ready to schedule (video/image + caption)
  • File saved in Drive with proper naming
  • Notion calendar updated with asset link

Quality Checks

  • No direct CTAs or promotional overlays remain
  • Caption follows DriveOn voice (L2-08)
  • Video has subtitles (if dialogue-heavy)
  • Aspect ratio correct for platform (9:16, 1:1, 4:5)
  • File under 100MB (platform limits)

  • ‚ùå Trend-chasing without brand fit (using trending audio that contradicts positioning)
üí° 2025 Best Practice: Engagement Rate Over Vanity Metrics

In 2025, algorithms prioritize engagement (saves, shares, meaningful comments) over raw reach. A post with 1,000 reach and 5% engagement beats 10,000 reach with 0.5% engagement. Focus on content that drives saves and shares‚Äîthese signal value to algorithms.

üö® Escalation & Decision Rights

Clear boundaries on what you own vs. what requires escalation. When in doubt, escalate early‚Äîespecially for brand or legal concerns.

Decisions You Own (No Escalation Needed)

  • Daily posting times and scheduling
  • Caption writing (as long as it follows L2-08 voice)
  • Hashtag selection
  • Comment/DM responses (standard engagement)
  • Content format choices (Reel vs Carousel vs Static)
  • Repurposing approach (how to adapt winning ads)
  • Weekly content calendar adjustments based on performance
  • Engagement tactics (likes, follows, community building)

Decisions Requiring Creative Director Approval

  • New brand channel setup (which winners get organic presence)
  • Major content strategy shifts (changing posting frequency, pivoting content mix)
  • Off-brand content requests (if uncertain about L2-08 compliance)
  • Influencer partnerships or UGC collaborations
  • Paid promotion of organic posts (boosting)
  • Brand voice questions (new product categories, sensitive topics)

Decisions Requiring PM or Founder Approval

  • New tool additions (scheduling platforms, analytics tools)
  • Budget requests (paid boosts, influencer payments)
  • Major crises (viral negative content, legal threats)
  • Cross-pod resource requests (need more creative assets than available)
