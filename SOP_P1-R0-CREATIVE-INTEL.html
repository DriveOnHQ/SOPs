üîÑ Core Workflows

These workflows define how you execute your daily work and hand off deliverables to downstream teams.

Workflow 1: Creative Pattern Discovery

Trigger: Daily routine + strategic niche request Frequency: 3-5x/day

Inputs

  • TikTok Creative Center feeds (trending ads)
  • Facebook Ads Library search results (high-engagement ads)
  • Minea platform data (dropshipping ad performance)
  • Strategic niche priorities from Lead Strategist

Steps

  1. Platform Scan: Open TikTok Creative Center, filter by "Last 7 Days" + relevant product categories. Note ads with 1M+ views, high engagement rates, or rapid growth trajectories.
  2. Hook Analysis: For each promising ad, identify the hook pattern (first 3 seconds). Categorize: Problem callout, Curiosity gap, Before/after, Trend/meme reference, Authority/social proof, or Pattern interrupt.
  3. Angle Extraction: Determine the core marketing angle: What desire/pain/outcome is being promised? How is it positioned (aspirational, practical, emotional, logical)?
  4. Format Documentation: Note the creative format: UGC-style, Professional talking head, Product demo, Lifestyle montage, Comparison, Tutorial/how-to, or Meme/trend hijack.
  5. Screenshot & Save: Capture key frames to Drive folder: /DriveOn Commerce/Product Research/Creative_Intel/[Date]/[Niche]. Include URL in filename for reference.
  6. Pattern Tagging: Tag with metadata: Hook_Type, Angle_Category, Format, Niche, Estimated_Engagement, Date_Captured, Platform_Source.

Outputs

  • Screenshots in Drive: /DriveOn Commerce/Product Research/Creative_Intel/
  • Pattern notes in Notion Creative Intel DB
  • Tagged categories for retrieval

Quality Checks

  • ‚úì Hook is clearly identifiable and transferable
  • ‚úì Angle maps to a real customer desire/pain
  • ‚úì Format is replicable within DriveOn budget constraints
  • ‚úì Examples are recent (last 30 days preferred)

Workflow 2: Intel Packet Creation

Trigger: After discovering 3-5 related patterns Frequency: 3-5 packets/day

Inputs

  • Collected creative patterns (screenshots + notes)
  • Platform performance data
  • Niche context from existing research

Steps

  1. Open Notion Template: Navigate to Notion Creative Intel DB. Click "New" and select "Creative Intel Packet Template".
  2. Fill Packet Header: Enter Packet_ID (auto-generated), Date, Your Name, Primary Niche, Status (Draft ‚Üí Review ‚Üí Complete).
  3. Document Hook Pattern: Write 2-3 sentence description of the hook pattern. Include specific examples: "This hook uses [specific technique] to achieve [specific outcome]. Example: '[verbatim hook text]'."
  4. Explain Angle Strategy: Describe the underlying marketing angle: "This angle targets [audience desire/pain] by positioning the product as [specific benefit/transformation]. It works because [psychological/emotional reason]."
  5. Detail Format Specs: Specify creative format requirements: Length (15s/30s/60s), Visual style, Audio/music requirements, Text overlay patterns, CTA structure.
  6. Link Swipe Files: Embed Drive links to 3-5 example screenshots. Use format: [Platform] - [Hook Type] - [Date] - [Performance Note]
  7. Add Best Practices: List 3-5 tactical best practices: "Use [specific technique] in first 3 seconds", "Avoid [common mistake]", "Test [specific variant]".
  8. Tag & Categorize: Add tags: Niche, Hook_Type, Angle_Category, Format, Confidence_Level (High/Medium/Low), Urgency (Trending/Evergreen).
  9. Quality Check: Review packet against checklist: Is hook actionable? Is angle clear? Are examples recent? Is it junior-executable?
  10. Submit for Review: Change status to "Review", assign to Lead Strategist or Creative Director, post link in #p1-research-updates.

Outputs

  • Completed Creative Intel Packet in Notion
  • ClickUp task updated to R0_INTEL_COMPLETE
  • Discord notification in #p1-research-updates

Quality Checks

  • ‚úì Hook, angle, and format are all documented
  • ‚úì 3-5 swipe file examples included
  • ‚úì Best practices are actionable and specific
  • ‚úì Tags match canonical categories
  • ‚úì Packet is junior-executable (clear enough for P2 to implement)

Workflow 3: Product-Creative Matching

Trigger: R0 product needs creative angle Frequency: 5-10x/week

Inputs

  • R0 product idea from ClickUp R0 Ideas list
  • Product category and positioning
  • Existing creative intel packets (Notion DB)

Steps

  1. Review Product Brief: Open ClickUp task for R0 product. Read product description, target audience, and any initial angle notes.
  2. Search Intel Library: Query Notion Creative Intel DB by niche, hook type, and angle category. Look for packets with similar product positioning or audience.
  3. Match 2-3 Angles: Select 2-3 creative intel packets that could apply to this product. Consider: Hook transferability, Angle alignment with product benefit, Format feasibility within P1 budget.
  4. Adapt Recommendations: For each matched packet, write specific adaptation notes: "Use [hook pattern] but emphasize [product-specific benefit]", "Replace [generic element] with [product-specific element]".
  5. Create Cross-Link: In Notion Creative Intel DB, add product to "Applied To Products" field. In ClickUp R0 task, add comment with links to recommended intel packets.
  6. Notify Downstream: Post in #p1-r0-idea-dump: "Creative intel ready for [Product_ID]: [Hook 1], [Hook 2], [Hook 3]. See ClickUp task for details."

Outputs

  • ClickUp comment linking to 2-3 creative intel packets
  • Adaptation notes for product-specific use
  • Discord notification with summary

Quality Checks

  • ‚úì 2-3 distinct angles recommended (not redundant)
  • ‚úì Adaptation notes are product-specific
  • ‚úì Formats are feasible within P1 creative budget (4-6 creatives)
  • ‚úì Examples are recent and relevant

ü§ñ AI Usage & Prompt Patterns

AI tools accelerate pattern analysis and documentation. Use these prompts as starting points.

AI Use Cases for This Role

  • Pattern Synthesis: Summarize 5-10 similar ads into a single hook pattern
  • Angle Extraction: Analyze ad copy to identify underlying marketing angles
  • Format Documentation: Convert video observation notes into structured format specs
  • Trend Analysis: Spot emerging creative patterns across multiple examples
  • Adaptation Suggestions: Generate product-specific variations of general patterns

Ready-to-Copy Prompts

Prompt 1: Hook Pattern Analysis

I'm analyzing winning TikTok/Meta ads for dropshipping products. I have 5 examples of high-performing ads in [NICHE]. Common elements I observe: - Hook: [Describe opening 3 seconds] - Angle: [Describe marketing angle] - Format: [Describe video style] Please help me: 1. Identify the core hook pattern (what makes it stop the scroll?) 2. Explain the underlying angle strategy (what desire/pain does it target?) 3. Write a transferable pattern description that could apply to similar products 4. Suggest 2-3 variants to test Keep the output junior-executable - a creative team should be able to implement this without asking clarifying questions.

Prompt 2: Swipe File Synthesis

I have 8 screenshots of winning ads in [NICHE] from the last 14 days. They all use similar hooks but with variations. For each ad, I'll provide: - Platform (TikTok/Meta) - Estimated views/engagement - Hook text (first 3 seconds) - Key visual elements [PASTE AD DETAILS] Please: 1. Identify the 2-3 most common hook patterns 2. Rate each pattern by estimated transferability (High/Medium/Low) 3. Suggest which pattern would work best for: [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION] 4. Write adaptation notes for product-specific implementation Output in Notion-friendly markdown with clear sections.

Prompt 3: Product-Angle Matching

I have a new product to test: [PRODUCT DESCRIPTION] Target audience: [AUDIENCE DESCRIPTION] Primary benefit: [BENEFIT DESCRIPTION] I have 3 creative intel packets I'm considering: 1. [PACKET 1: Hook type + Angle summary] 2. [PACKET 2: Hook type + Angle summary] 3. [PACKET 3: Hook type + Angle summary] Please: 1. Rank these packets by fit for this specific product (1 = best fit) 2. For the top 2 packets, write specific adaptation notes 3. Identify any gaps: what hook/angle is missing that might work well? 4. Suggest a creative testing strategy: which 2-3 angles to test first in P1? Keep recommendations within budget: 4-6 creatives for P1, $40-$60 spend.

üö® Escalation & Decision Rights

Know what you can decide independently vs. what requires escalation.

Decisions You Can Make Alone

  • Which ads/platforms to scan daily (within strategic niche priorities)
  • How to categorize hooks, angles, and formats in your taxonomy
  • Which patterns to document in intel packets
  • How to organize swipe files in Drive
  • How many examples to include per packet (3-5 is baseline)
  • Whether a pattern is "trending" vs "evergreen"
  • Daily prioritization of intel tasks within your WIP cap

Decisions Requiring Lead Strategist Approval

  • Strategic niche priorities (which categories to focus scanning)
  • New hook/angle taxonomy categories (if existing tags don't fit)
  • Intel packets that recommend budget changes or gate modifications
  • Recommending products skip R1/R2 based purely on creative potential
  • Access requests to new paid intel tools beyond approved stack

Decisions Requiring Creative Director Involvement

  • Creative format recommendations that deviate from P1 budget constraints
  • Brand compliance questions (e.g., "Is this hook too aggressive?")
  • Major creative strategy shifts (e.g., "Should we pivot to X format?")
  • Intel packets with conflicting angle recommendations

When to Escalate (Immediate)

‚ö†Ô∏è Escalate Immediately If:
  • You discover platform policy changes that affect our creative strategy
  • A major competitor pivots to a new format/angle we're not tracking
  • Your WIP consistently exceeds 10 for 3+ days (capacity issue)
  • Downstream teams (P1/P2) repeatedly reject or ignore your intel packets
  • You spot a compliance risk (e.g., ads using prohibited claims)
  • Access to core tools (Minea, TikTok, FB Library) is blocked/limited
